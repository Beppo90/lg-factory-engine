He works at DevCore Solutions. He has a problem with his workstation — and he needs to write a request to IT. Your job: understand what Carlos needs, decide if you would approve his request, and learn the structures he uses so YOU can write your own email later."*

### BLOQUE A — PRE-READING: TOOLBELT (20 min) — 0:15 a 0:35

**Worksheet:** PM-2.3, The Toolbelt section
**Agrupación:** Plenary → Individual → Plenary
**Objetivo del bloque:** Los aprendices conocen las 5 palabras clave antes de leer, lo que reduce la carga cognitiva durante la lectura.

---

#### Toolbelt: Introducción (10 min) — 0:15 a 0:25

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. Proyectar slide 5 (Toolbelt words con definiciones).
2. Instructor presenta cada palabra, una por una. Para CADA palabra:
   - Dice la palabra en voz alta: *"The first word is SPEC SHEET."*
   - Da la definición: *"A Spec Sheet is a document with technical information about a product."*
   - Da un ejemplo contextualizado: *"Example: 'I need the spec sheet for this computer — I want to know the CPU and RAM.'"*
   - Los aprendices repiten la palabra 2 veces en voz alta (choral repetition).

3. Las 5 palabras en orden:
   1. **Spec Sheet** — *"A document with technical information about a product"*
   2. **Upgrade** — *"To change a part for a better one"*
   3. **Compatible** — *"When two parts can work together"*
   4. **Performance** — *"How fast or how well something works"*
   5. **Budget** — *"The money available to buy something"*

4. Después de las 5: *"Now — open your PM-2.3 worksheet. Look at The Toolbelt at the top. You see the 5 words. Write a simple example sentence for EACH word. You have 5 minutes."*
5. Timer: 5 minutos. Individual.

💡 *Los aprendices A1 pueden tener dificultad con "compatible" y "performance". Para "compatible", use gesto de encajar piezas de rompecabezas. Para "performance", señale un computador y dige: "Fast performance = good. Slow performance = bad."*

#### Toolbelt: Quick Check (10 min) — 0:25 a 0:35

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Ok, let's check. I say the DEFINITION — you say the WORD. Ready?"*
2. Instructor dice cada definición, los aprendices responden en coro:
   - *"A document with technical information about a product"* → "Spec Sheet!"
   - *"To change a part for a better one"* → "Upgrade!"
   - *"When two parts can work together"* → "Compatible!"
   - *"How fast or how well something works"* → "Performance!"
   - *"The money available to buy something"* → "Budget!"

3. *"Good. Now I say the WORD — you give me a quick example. 'Spec Sheet' — what's an example?"* — 2-3 voluntarios responden.
4. Repetir para las 5 palabras.

✓ **Checkpoint:** *"Write ONE sentence using the word 'upgrade'. Show me on your paper. You have 1 minute."*
- Timer: 1 minuto. Instructor circula rápido y verifica que >70% tiene algo escrito.

**Transition → Bloque B:**
*"Good. You know the 5 key words. Now let's read Carlos's email. These words are ALL in the email."*

### BLOQUE B — WHILE-READING (50 min) — 0:35 a 1:25

**Worksheet:** PM-2.3, Activities 1 + 2 + gist reading
**Agrupación:** Individual → Pairs → Plenary
**Objetivo del bloque:** Los aprendices comprenden el email de Carlos, extraen datos específicos en una tabla, y verifican comprensión con un checkpoint.

---

#### First Read: Gist (15 min) — 0:35 a 0:50

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. Proyectar slide 6 (email de Carlos en pantalla).
2. *"Now — read this email. It's from Carlos. He's writing to IT Support. Read it silently. Don't answer any questions yet — just READ. You have 5 minutes."*
3. Timer: 5 minutos. Individual. Silencio.

**El email (para referencia del instructor):**

> **INTERNAL TECH REQUEST — DevCore Solutions**
> **From:** Carlos Ramírez — Junior Developer
> **To:** IT Department — Hardware Support
> **Subject:** Upgrade Request for Workstation #07
>
> Hello,
>
> My workstation **has** a problem. The **CPU is** old. It **is** an Intel i3 processor. It **is** very slow for compiling code. The **RAM is** only 4 GB. **This is** not enough for running the development tools.
>
> The current **HDD is** a 500 GB mechanical drive. It **is** slow. I need a **SSD**. An SSD **is** faster than an HDD.
>
> The **monitor is** small. It only **has** a VGA port. I need a monitor that **has** an HDMI port. **This is** important for connecting **the GPU** to an external display.
>
> The **keyboard is** compatible, but the **mouse has** a broken scroll wheel.
>
> **My request:** I need a new CPU (Intel i5 or higher), 16 GB of RAM, a 1 TB SSD, an HDMI monitor, and a new mouse. The total budget **is** $1,200.
>
> Thank you,
> Carlos Ramírez

4. *"Done reading? With your partner — answer this question: What does Carlos want? One sentence. You have 2 minutes."*
5. Timer: 2 minutos. Pairs.
6. *"Who can answer? What does Carlos want?"* — 2-3 voluntarios.
7. Respuesta esperada: *"He wants to upgrade his workstation / He needs new parts for his computer."*

💡 *Es normal que aprendices A1 no comprendan todo el email en la primera lectura. No se preocupe — la segunda lectura (con la tabla) es donde construyen la comprensión detallada.*

---

#### Second Read: Information Transfer (35 min) — 0:50 a 1:25

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Now — read the email AGAIN. This time, you have a TASK. Look at Activity 1 on your worksheet: the Information Transfer table. You need to fill in TWO columns: Current Spec (what Carlos has NOW) and Requested Spec (what Carlos WANTS). For each component."*
2. *"There are 5 components in the table: CPU, RAM, Storage, Monitor, Mouse. Find the information in the email and write it. DO NOT translate — extract the data. Use the EXACT words from the email."*
3. *"You have 10 minutes. Go."*
4. Timer: 10 minutos. Individual. Silencio.

**Answer Key (in-line — para referencia del instructor mientras circula):**

| Component | Current Spec | Requested Spec |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| CPU | Intel i3 (old, very slow for compiling) | Intel i5 or higher |
| RAM | 4 GB | 16 GB |
| Storage | 500 GB HDD (mechanical, slow) | 1 TB SSD |
| Monitor | Small, only VGA port | HDMI monitor |
| Mouse | Broken scroll wheel | New mouse |

**Total Budget:** $1,200

5. *"Time. Turn to your partner. Compare your table. Do you have the same answers? If different — check the email together. You have 5 minutes."*
6. Timer: 5 minutos. Pairs. Instructor circula y escucha discrepancias.

7. *"Ok, let's check together. Eyes on me."* — Revisar la tabla completa en plenaria, componente por componente:
   - *"CPU — what does Carlos have NOW?"* → "Intel i3"
   - *"And what does he WANT?"* → "Intel i5 or higher"
   - Repetir para RAM, Storage, Monitor, Mouse.
   - *"Total budget?"* → "$1,200"
   - Instructor escribe las respuestas en LIVE ZONE del tablero.

💡 *El error más común: los aprendices escriben "4" en lugar de "4 GB" para RAM, o "500" en lugar de "500 GB HDD" para Storage. Insista en que escriban la unidad. Esto es ESP — las unidades importan en comunicación técnica.*

✓ **Checkpoint (5 min) — 1:25 a 1:30**

*"Quick check — look at your partner's table. Do you BOTH have 5 components filled in? Raise your hand if yes."*

Si <80% levanta la mano: *"Ok, 2 more minutes — fill in any blanks. Use the email."* Timer: 2 minutos.

Si >80% levanta la mano: *"Good. You understand what Carlos needs. Now — YOU decide."*

**Transition → BREAK:**
*"Take a break. 15 minutes. Come back ready to make a tough decision — you're the IT Manager, and you don't have enough money."*

- Qué componentes causaron más confusión en la tabla (para aclarar después del break)
- Qué aprendices parecen tener más dificultad con la lectura (para apoyo en Bloque D)

### BLOQUE C — POST-READING (40 min) — 1:45 a 2:25

**Worksheet:** PM-2.3, Activity 2
**Agrupación:** Plenary → Individual → Groups of 4 → Plenary
**Objetivo del bloque:** Los aprendices aplican pensamiento crítico al priorizar upgrades con presupuesto limitado, justificando con evidencia del texto.

---

#### Transición (5 min) — 1:45 a 1:50

**Teacher Talk — Transition:**
*"Welcome back. You know what Carlos needs. Five upgrades, $1,200 total. But here's the problem: you are the IT Manager. Your budget is $800. Not $1,200. You can't approve everything. You have to choose."*

1. Proyectar slide 7 (escenario del presupuesto).
2. Escribir en el tablero: **Budget: $800 (not $1,200)**

---

#### Technical Decision (15 min) — 1:50 a 2:05

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Look at Activity 2 on your worksheet. You see 5 options. Your budget is $800. Which TWO upgrades are the MOST important? Circle your choices — mark Approve ✓ or Reject ✗ for EACH one."*
2. *"You have 5 minutes to decide. ALONE. No talking."*
3. Timer: 5 minutos. Individual.

4. *"Now — the second part. Under the table, write your justification. 'I approve _______ because the text says: ________________.' You must use a sentence FROM THE EMAIL to justify. No opinions — evidence."*
5. *"You have 5 minutes."*
6. Timer: 5 minutos. Individual.

**Answer Key (in-line):**
No hay una respuesta "correcta" única. Sin embargo, las respuestas más defendibles con evidencia del texto son:
- **CPU**: "It is very slow for compiling code." / "The CPU is old."
- **RAM**: "The RAM is only 4 GB. This is not enough for running the development tools."
- **SSD**: "The HDD is a 500 GB mechanical drive. It is slow." / "An SSD is faster than an HDD."

El mouse y el monitor son menos urgentes según el texto (el mouse tiene scroll roto pero funciona; el monitor tiene VGA pero funciona).

💡 *NO diga a los aprendices cuál es la "respuesta correcta". La actividad es de pensamiento crítico — cualquier elección es válida SI se justifica con texto. La calidad de la justificación es lo que se evalúa.*

---

#### Debate en grupos de 4 (20 min) — 2:05 a 2:25

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Now — join the pair behind you. You are now a group of 4."*
2. Transición: 1 minuto para formar grupos de 4.
3. *"In your group: share your TWO choices. Do you all agree? Probably not. Discuss. Each person explains WHY they chose what they chose. Use the sentence from the email."*
4. *"You have 8 minutes. At the end, your group must agree on TWO upgrades. Write the group decision on ONE paper."*
5. Timer: 8 minutos. Groups of 4. Instructor circula.

6. *"Time. Now let's hear from some groups. Group 2 — what did you choose and why?"*
7. 2-3 grupos comparten su decisión y justificación (2 min cada uno).
8. Después de cada presentación, instructor pregunta a la clase: *"Do you agree? Why or why not?"* — Breve intercambio (1 min).

💡 *Los debates más productivos ocurren cuando los grupos tienen opiniones diferentes. Si todos los grupos eligieron lo mismo, pregunte: "Did anyone want to approve the monitor? Why?" Esto abre la discusión.*

**Transition → Bloque D:**
*"Good decisions. Now — Carlos used some very useful sentences in his email. Let's find them."*

---

### BLOQUE D — DESTRUCTURE FOR FUTURE OUTPUT (15 min) — 2:35 a 2:50

**Worksheet:** PM-2.3, Activity 3
**Agrupación:** Individual → Plenary
**Objetivo del bloque:** Los aprendices identifican las 3 estructuras funcionales del email (describe problem, request, compare) como "formulas" que reutilizarán en Session 5.

---

#### Encontrar las funciones (10 min) — 2:35 a 2:45

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Last activity with the email. Look at Activity 3. There are 3 functions: describe a problem, request something, compare two things. Find the EXACT sentence from the email that Carlos used for EACH function."*
2. *"Copy the sentence WORD FOR WORD from the email. Don't invent — find it."*
3. *"You have 5 minutes."*
4. Timer: 5 minutos. Individual.

**Answer Key (in-line):**

| Function | Exact sentence from the text |
|----------|------------------------------|
| **Describe a problem** | "The CPU is old." / "It is very slow for compiling code." / "The RAM is only 4 GB." (cualquiera de estas es válida) |
| **Request something** | "I need a new CPU (Intel i5 or higher), 16 GB of RAM, a 1 TB SSD, an HDMI monitor, and a new mouse." |
| **Compare two things** | "An SSD is faster than an HDD." |

5. *"Let's check. What sentence did you find for 'describe a problem'?"* — 2-3 voluntarios comparten. Instructor confirma las respuestas correctas.

---

## DIFFERENTIATION NOTES

### Fast Finishers

- **Activity 1 (Information Transfer):** Si completa la tabla en <5 minutos: *"Write 1 additional row: What would YOU request for YOUR workstation? Component, current spec, requested spec."*
- **Activity 2 (Technical Decision):** Si termina antes: *"Now — what if the budget is $1,000 instead of $800? Which THREE would you approve? Write a new justification."*
- **Activity 3 (Deconstruct):** Si termina antes: *"Write 3 NEW sentences using the same formulas — but about a phone (not a computer). Example: 'The screen is small. It has a broken camera. I need a new phone.'"*
- **Peer tutor role:** *"Help your partner find the sentences in Activity 3. Point to the email — don't give the answer."*

### More Support Needed

- **Toolbelt:** Permitir que el aprendiz mantenga el Toolbelt visible durante toda la sesión (no lo guarde). Es un andaje.
- **Activity 1 (Information Transfer):** Proporcionar una versión del email con las respuestas SUBRAYADAS (preparar 2-3 copias). El aprendiz lee la versión subrayada y copia — el objetivo es que practique la transferencia de datos, no la búsqueda exhaustiva.

## SET-UP DETALLADO (15 min) — 0:00 a 0:15

### Warm-up: "Formula Recall" (10 min)

**Paso 1 — Recreate from memory (5 min)**

1. Los aprendices entran. Instructor dice:
*"Good morning everyone. Take out a clean piece of paper. No worksheets — just paper."*

2. *"Last session we learned 3 grammar structures. From MEMORY — write the 3 formulas. No looking at your PM-2.7. You have 2 minutes."*
3. Timer: 2 minutos. Individual. Silencio.

**Paso 2 — Volunteers on board (5 min)**

4. *"Who can write Formula 1 on the board?"* — 1 voluntario escribe: "[Component] + IS + [definition/adjective]"
5. *"Formula 2?"* — 1 voluntario escribe: "[Device] + HAS + [feature]"
6. *"Formula 3?"* — 1 voluntario escribe: "THIS/THAT + IS / THESE/THOSE + ARE"
7. Instructor confirma o corrige cada fórmula.

8. *"Now — which sentences from Carlos's email used these structures? Quick — tell your partner. 1 minute."*
9. Timer: 1 minuto. Pairs.
10. *"One example?"* — 1 voluntario: "The CPU is old" / "It has 4 GB of RAM" / "This is not enough"

💡 *Si los aprendices no recuerdan las fórmulas, no se preocupe — el Bloque A las retoma con el Blueprint Model. El warm-up es activación, no evaluación.*

### Teacher Talk — Opening Script (3 min)

*"Good morning everyone. You've read Carlos's email. You've listened to his phone call. You've learned the vocabulary, pronunciation, and grammar. TODAY — you write your own Tech Request email. This is where everything comes together. You are a developer at SENA. Your workstation has a problem. You write to IT. In English. Let's go."*

### Objective (escrito en tablero)

*"Today you will: write your own Tech Request email in English — using the structures, vocabulary, and format you've learned."*

**ICQ:**
- *"Are we reading an email today or writing one?"* → Esperan: "Writing"
- *"In what language?"* → Esperan: "English"

### BLOQUE A — DECONSTRUCT (30 min) — 0:15 a 0:45

**Worksheet:** PM-2.4, Activity 1 + Blueprint Model
**Agrupación:** Plenary → Individual → Pairs → Plenary
**Objetivo del bloque:** Los aprendices analizan la estructura pragmática del email de Carlos para identificar las convenciones de género que replicarán en su propia escritura.

---

#### Blueprint Model: Display (5 min) — 0:15 a 0:20

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. Proyectar slide 23 (Blueprint Model — versión simplificada del email de Carlos).
2. Instructor lee el email en voz alta, señalando cada parte en la pantalla:

*"From: Carlos Ramírez — Junior Developer. To: IT Department. Subject: Upgrade Request for Workstation #07. Hello. My workstation has a problem. The CPU is old. It is very slow for compiling code. I need a new CPU, 16 GB of RAM, and a 1 TB SSD. Thank you. Carlos Ramírez."*

3. *"This is Carlos's email. SHORT. CLEAR. PROFESSIONAL. Today you write one just like this — but about YOUR computer. Let's first understand HOW it's organized."*

---

#### Activity 1 — Pragmatic Analysis (20 min) — 0:20 a 0:40

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Activity 1. There are 5 parts of the email. Match each part with its PURPOSE. You have 3 minutes."*
2. Timer: 3 minutos. Individual.

**Answer Key (in-line):**

| Part of the email | Purpose |
|-------------------|---------|
| 1. "Hello," | C. Greeting |
| 2. "My workstation has a problem." | D. Introduce the problem |
| 3. "The CPU is old. It is very slow." | A. Describe the problem |
| 4. "I need a new CPU, 16 GB of RAM..." | B. Request what you need |
| 5. "Thank you," | E. Close politely |

3. *"Compare with your partner. Same answers? 2 minutes."*
4. Timer: 2 minutos. Pairs.
5. *"Let's check. 'Hello,' — what purpose?"* → "C. Greeting"
6. Revisar los 5 ítems. Instructor confirma.

7. *"Now — IMPORTANT question. Why does the email start with the problem, NOT with 'How are you?' Talk with your partner. 1 minute."*
8. Timer: 1 minuto. Pairs.
9. *"Why?"* — 2-3 voluntarios.
10. Instructor explica: *"Because it's a TECH REQUEST, not a social email. The IT person is busy. They want to know: WHAT IS THE PROBLEM? So you start with the problem. Greeting — Problem — Request — Closing. That's the structure. Simple."*

11. Instructor escribe la estructura en el tablero (Email Structure zone):
```
Greeting → Problem → Details → Request → Closing
```

💡 *Esta es la convención de género más importante. Los aprendices hispanohablantes suelen escribir emails largos con "Espero que estés bien" y contexto innecesario. En un Tech Request en inglés: directo al problema. Refuerce: "In English tech emails — SHORT. Problem first. Always."*

---

#### Pregunta de discusión + plenaria (10 min) — 0:40 a 0:45

*"Look at the KEY STRUCTURES in the email (in bold on your worksheet). Carlos uses: 'My workstation HAS a problem.' 'The CPU IS old.' 'I NEED a new CPU.' 'This IS not enough.' These are YOUR writing tools. You'll use the SAME structures."*

**Transition → BREAK:**
*"Good analysis. Take a break — 15 minutes. Come back ready to practice writing before the real thing."*

### BLOQUE B — GUIDED DRAFTING (35 min) — 1:00 a 1:35

**Worksheet:** PM-2.4, Activity 2
**Agrupación:** Plenary → Individual → Plenary
**Objetivo del bloque:** Los aprendices practican el formato escribiendo un email guiado (con blanks) antes de escribir el suyo propio.

---

#### Transición (5 min) — 1:00 a 1:05

**Teacher Talk — Transition:**
*"Welcome back. You know the structure. Now let's PRACTICE filling it in — before you write from scratch. Different developer, different problem. You fill in the blanks."*

---

#### Fill the Skeleton (30 min) — 1:05 a 1:35

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. Proyectar slide 24 (Activity 2 — Fill the Skeleton).
2. *"Activity 2. A different developer has a problem. Read the context:"*

**Contexto:**
*"Another developer — the monitor is old. It only has a VGA port. The mouse is broken. You need to complete the email using the Language Bank: has, is, need, not compatible, is old, This is."*

3. *"Fill in the blanks. Use the Language Bank. You have 8 minutes."*
4. Timer: 8 minutos. Individual.

**El skeleton email (para referencia):**

> **From:** [Your Name] — Junior Developer
> **To:** IT Department
> **Subject:** Upgrade Request for Workstation #___
>
> Hello,
>
> My workstation _______ a problem. The monitor _______ _______ . It only _______ a VGA port. _______ _______ _______ with the new GPU.
>
> The keyboard _______ OK, but the mouse _______ broken.
>
> I _______ a new monitor with HDMI and a new mouse.
>
> Thank you,
> _______________________

**Answer Key (in-line):**

| Blank | Answer |
|-------|--------|
| 1. "My workstation _______ a problem" | **has** |
| 2. "The monitor _______" | **is** |
| 3. "_______" (second blank in monitor line) | **old** |
| 4. "It only _______ a VGA port" | **has** |
| 5. "_______ _______ _______ with the new GPU" | **This is not compatible** |
| 6. "The keyboard _______ OK" | **is** |
| 7. "the mouse _______ broken" | **is** |
| 8. "I _______ a new monitor" | **need** |

5. *"Let's check together."* — Revisar blank por blank en plenaria. Instructor escribe respuestas en LIVE ZONE.

6. *"Now — read the COMPLETE email aloud to your partner. 1 minute."*
7. Timer: 1 minuto. Pairs.

8. Instructor revisa 2-3 ejemplos con la clase. Para cada ejemplo:
   - Lee el email completo en voz alta
   - Pregunta: *"Is the grammar correct? Is the structure correct?"*
   - Si hay error: corrige colectivamente

💡 *Este ejercicio es PUENTE. Los aprendices practican el formato con soporte (Language Bank) antes de escribir libremente. Si un aprendiz no puede completar el skeleton, es señal de que necesita más apoyo en Bloque C.*

**Transition → Bloque C:**
*"You filled in the blanks. Now — the real thing. YOUR email. YOUR computer. YOUR problem."*

### BLOQUE C — THE FINAL TASK (50 min) — 1:35 a 2:25

**Worksheet:** PM-2.4, Activity 3
**Agrupación:** Individual (instructor circulates)
**Objetivo del bloque:** Los aprendices escriben un email completo de Tech Request sobre un computador real que usan, aplicando todas las estructuras, vocabulario y formato aprendidos.

---

#### Instructions + Writing Time (45 min) — 1:35 a 2:20

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. Proyectar slide 25 (Activity 3 — Your Own Tech Request).
2. *"NOW it's YOUR turn. Write YOUR OWN Tech Request. Think about a REAL computer you use — at SENA, at home, or at work. What is the problem? What do you need?"*
3. *"The email must have: From, To, Subject, Greeting, Problem description, Request, Closing."*
4. *"Use the Writing Formulas on the board. Use the vocabulary from the Vocab Wall. Use the grammar structures from Session 4."*
5. *"You have 30 minutes to write. ALONE. No talking. Go."*
6. Timer: 30 minutos. Individual. Silencio. Instructor circula.

**Mientras circula, el instructor:**
- Lee por encima el hombro (sin interrumpir)
- Ayuda a aprendices que están bloqueados: *"What computer do you use? What's wrong with it? Start with: 'My workstation has a problem.'"*
- Toma nota mental de errores comunes (para corregir en Peer Review o plenaria)
- NO corrige durante la escritura — deja que fluya

💡 *Los aprendices A1 pueden bloquearse ante la "página en blanco." Si un aprendiz no ha escrito nada después de 5 minutos, acérquese y dígale: "Start with the Subject line. What workstation number?" — Una vez que empieza el formato, el contenido fluye.*

7. *"Time. Now — the Auditor's Checklist. Look at the bottom of your worksheet. Self-check BEFORE you give it to your partner. 2 minutes."*
8. Timer: 2 minutos. Individual.

**El Auditor's Checklist (PM-2.4):**
- [ ] I used **"is"** to describe a problem (Ej: "The CPU is old")
- [ ] I used **"has"** to describe a feature (Ej: "It has a VGA port")
- [ ] I used **"I need"** to make my request
- [ ] I included the **Subject line** with the workstation number
- [ ] My email has: **Greeting → Problem → Details → Request → Closing**

✓ **Checkpoint:** *"Raise your hand if ALL 5 items on the checklist are checked."* — Contar manos.

---

#### Peer Review (15 min) — 2:20 a 2:35

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Exchange your email with your partner. Read their email. Then check the Auditor's Checklist for THEM. 3 minutes to read."*
2. Timer: 3 minutos. Pairs. (Cada uno lee el email del otro.)

3. *"Now — give feedback. TWO things:"*
   - *"Thing 1: ONE positive comment — what did they do WELL?"*
   - *"Thing 2: ONE suggestion — what could they IMPROVE?"*
4. *"Write your feedback at the bottom of their paper. 2 minutes."*
5. Timer: 2 minutos. Pairs.

6. *"Give the paper back. Read your partner's feedback. 1 minute."*
7. Timer: 1 minuto.

8. *"Who got useful feedback? Share — what did your partner say you did well?"* — 2-3 voluntarios.
9. *"What suggestion did you get?"* — 2-3 voluntarios.

💡 *El Peer Review es formativo, no sumativo. Refuerce: "This is not a grade. This is help. Your partner is helping you make your email BETTER before you submit it."*

---

#### ✓ Checkpoint: Submit Draft (5 min) — 2:35 a 2:40

1. *"Last step. Based on your partner's feedback — do you want to make ONE quick change? You have 2 minutes."*
2. Timer: 2 minutos. Individual. (Cambios menores — no reescribir.)

3. *"Now — submit your final draft to me. Hand it in."*
4. Instructor recoge los drafts. (Revisar después de clase con el Answer Key consolidado.)

**Transition → WRAP-UP:**
*"Good work. Let's close."*

### Exit Ticket (8 min) — 2:40 a 2:48

**Instrucciones:**
1. *"On a clean piece of paper — write ONE sentence from your email that uses 'is' and ONE sentence that uses 'has.' The exact sentences you wrote."*
2. *"You have 2 minutes."*
3. Timer: 2 minutos. Individual.
4. *"Show your partner. Are the sentences grammatically correct? Quick check. 30 seconds."*
5. Timer: 30 segundos. Pairs.

**Criterio de éxito:** El aprendiz escribe 2 oraciones de su propio email: una con "is" y una con "has", ambas sobre hardware, ambas gramaticalmente coherentes.

### Teacher Talk — Closing Script (5 min) — 2:48 a 2:53

*"Excellent work today. You wrote a real email. In English. About a real computer with a real problem. That's a real skill — and you did it.*

*Your homework: Workbook Chapter 5 — Writing Draft. Two things: (1) revise your Tech Request based on your partner's feedback — write a clean second draft. (2) Practice reading it aloud 3 times for fluency. You need it for next session. 60 minutes.*

*Next session — you SPEAK. The Help Desk simulation. You call IT Support. You describe your problem. You use the phrases you learned in Session 3. Come ready to talk."*

**ICQ sobre el autónomo:**
- *"What do you do first?"* → Esperan: "Revise my email — write a clean draft"
- *"What do you do second?"* → Esperan: "Read it aloud 3 times"
- *"Why do I read it aloud?"* → Esperan: "For the speaking simulation next session"

## DIFFERENTIATION NOTES

### Fast Finishers

- **Activity 2 (Fill the Skeleton):** Si completa en <5 minutos: *"Write a SECOND skeleton email for a different problem — the GPU is old and the Ethernet cable is broken. New blanks, same structure."*
- **Activity 3 (Final Task):** Si termina el email en <20 minutos: *"Write a SECOND email — this time, you are the IT MANAGER responding to Carlos's original email. Approve or reject his request. Use: 'Your request is approved' or 'I can only approve [X] and [Y].'"*
- **Peer tutor role:** *"Help your partner improve their email. Ask questions: 'What is the SPECIFIC problem? Which component?' Don't write for them — guide them."*
- **Extra challenge:** *"Add a budget line to your email: 'The total cost is $______.' Research real prices online."*

### More Support Needed

- **Activity 2 (Fill the Skeleton):** Proporcionar el Language Bank como tarjeta física que el aprendiz mantiene sobre su escritorio. Permitir que consulte las Writing Formulas del tablero durante toda la actividad.
- **Activity 3 (Final Task):** Proporcionar un "Email Template" con la estructura completa pre-escrita. El aprendiz solo necesita llenar los espacios:
  ```
  From: _______ — ADSO Apprentice
  To: IT Department — SENA
  Subject: Upgrade Request for Workstation #___
  
  Hello,
  My workstation _______ a problem. The _______ is _______.
  It _______ _______.
  I need _______.
  Thank you,
  _______
  ```
- **Si el aprendiz no puede escribir después de 10 minutos:** Permítele usar el email de Activity 2 como MODELO. Cambie los detalles (otro componente, otro problema) pero mantenga la estructura. Esto es "guided writing" — no es plagio, es andamiaje.
- **Peer Review:** Emparejar aprendices de nivel similar. Un aprendiz A1.1 dando feedback a un A1.2 avanzado puede ser frustrante para ambos.

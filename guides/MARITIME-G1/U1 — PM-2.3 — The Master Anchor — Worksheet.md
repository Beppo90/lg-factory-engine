### 📰 LIFEBOATS: THE #1 REASON SHIPS ARE DETAINED

*Adapted from: Safety4Sea, "PSC Focus: Lifeboats remain a challenging item on ship inspections" — December 11, 2025*

**The Problem**

Lifeboats and rescue boats are the most common reason for ship detentions in Port State Control (PSC) inspections. The deficiency code 11101 — "Lifeboats" — is the code most frequently associated with detentions worldwide. This code covers eighteen (18) different problems.

**Why are lifeboats so problematic?**

During inspections, the PSC inspector checks not only the CONDITION of the equipment but also its OPERATIONAL READINESS and the CREW'S FAMILIARITY with its use.

**Real problems found on ships:**

Here are real deficiency findings from recent PSC inspections:

1. **Securing painter missing** — The rope that connects the lifeboat to the ship is not there.

2. **Release device not reset** — The mechanism that launches the lifeboat is in the wrong position. The crew cannot release the boat in an emergency.

3. **Crew seat belts taped** — The safety belts inside the lifeboat are covered with tape. Crew members cannot use them safely during launch.

4. **Lifeboat pins seized** — The metal pins that connect the boat to the davit are stuck. They do not move. The boat cannot launch.

5. **Launching means not tested** — Nobody tested if the lifeboat can actually go into the water.

6. **Equipment not marked** — The lifeboat does not have the required identification markings.

7. **Motor does not start** — The engine that propels the lifeboat does not work.

8. **Painter length incorrect** — The rope is too short or too long for safe deployment.

**The Inspector's Checklist**

The PSC inspector checks these areas of the ship during a life-saving appliances inspection:

| Area of the Ship | Equipment Inspected |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Bridge** | Distress signals, communication equipment, alarms |
| **Deck / Superstructure** | Lifeboats, rescue boats, davits, launching arrangements |
| **Engine Room** | Emergency generator, emergency fire pump |
| **Accommodation** | Life jackets, life rafts, muster list |
| **All areas** | Fire extinguishers, fire doors, escape routes |

**What the crew must know:**

The inspector asks the crew questions during the inspection:

- *"Show me how to release the lifeboat."*
- *"Where are the fire extinguishers on this deck?"*
- *"What is this equipment?"* (points to a davit, a painter, a hydrostatic release unit)
- *"How many lifeboats does this vessel carry?"*
- *"When was the last launching test?"*

**The crew must answer in English.** If they cannot name the equipment or explain the procedures, the inspector records a deficiency. Multiple deficiencies can lead to DETENTION — the ship cannot leave port.

**The lesson is clear:** A seafarer who cannot name ship parts and safety equipment in English puts the ship, the crew, and the cargo at risk.

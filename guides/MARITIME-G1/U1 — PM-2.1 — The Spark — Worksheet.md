## 📋 ACTIVITY 2 — ENGAGE: El debate por equipos (20 min — Teams)

*Form two teams. One team says YES. The other says NO.*

**Team YES** 🟢 — *"A seafarer MUST know ship parts in English."*

**Team NO** 🔴 — *"Spanish is enough. English is not necessary."*

### Rules:
- Each team has 3 minutes to prepare 3 arguments
- Each argument must use: *"I think..."* or *"In my opinion..."* or *"Because..."*
- Team YES speaks first (2 min). Then Team NO (2 min).
- Then open debate: 1 minute each team for rebuttal

### Useful phrases:

| Agreeing | Disagreeing | Giving reasons |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| I agree because... | I disagree because... | Because... |
| Yes, that's true, but... | No, that's not right because... | For example... |
| You are right about... | I don't think so because... | The reason is... |

**After the debate — VOTE:**

☐ Team YES convinced me.
☐ Team NO convinced me.
☐ I'm still not sure.

### 📰 THE NEWS

#### "Lifeboats: The #1 Reason Ships Are Detained"

*Source: Safety4Sea, December 2025*

> In 2025, **lifeboats and rescue boats** are the most common reason for ship detentions in Port State Control (PSC) inspections worldwide. The code "11101 — Lifeboats" is the deficiency code most frequently associated with detentions.
>
> Real problems found during inspections:
> - Securing painter **missing**
> - Release device not in the correct **position**
> - Crew seat belts **taped** — crew cannot use them safely
> - Both lifeboats' pins **seized** (stuck)
> - Launching means **not tested**
>
> When an inspector boards a ship and asks *"Where are the lifeboats?"* or *"Show me the fire extinguishers"* — the crew must answer **in English**. If they cannot name the equipment, the ship can be **detained**.

## FUNCTIONS FOR UNIT 1: SHIP OVERVIEW

### FUNCTION 1: DESCRIBING

**What it is:** Saying what something is and what it is like.

**Key structures:**
```
Subject + is + complement         → "The hull is steel."
Subject + are + complement        → "The lifeboats are on the deck."
This/That + is + noun             → "This is the bridge."
These/Those + are + noun          → "Those are fire extinguishers."
```

**Maritime examples:**
- "The bridge is on the superstructure."
- "The hull is steel."
- "The deck is the working surface of the ship."
- "The lifeboats are orange."
- "The gangway is on the starboard side."

**Injected in:**
- Session 2 (Reading) — describing what is seen in the PSC article
- Session 3 (Listening) — describing parts during the audio tour
- Session 4 (Grammar) — controlled practice of describing
- Session 5 (Writing) — describing sections in the Safety Equipment Checklist

---

### FUNCTION 2: CLASSIFYING

**What it is:** Saying what category something belongs to.

**Key structures:**
```
This/That + is + a + noun     → "This is a container ship."
Subject + is + a + noun       → "MV Caribbean Star is a container ship."
```

**Maritime examples:**
- "This is a container ship."
- "That is a bulk carrier."
- "MV Caribbean Star is a tanker."
- "The lifeboat is safety equipment."
- "The fire extinguisher is safety equipment."
- "The bridge is command equipment."

**Injected in:**
- Session 2 (Reading) — classifying equipment in the inspection checklist
- Session 3 (Listening) — identifying types of vessels
- Session 4 (Grammar) — classifying ship parts by category
- Session 5 (Writing) — classifying items in the Safety Equipment Checklist

---

### FUNCTION 3: LOCATING

**What it is:** Saying where something is.

**Key structures:**
```
Subject + is + prepositional phrase     → "The bridge is on the superstructure."
Subject + is + on/in/at + location      → "Lifeboats are on the deck."
The _____ is on the _____ side.         → "The gangway is on the starboard side."
The _____ is below/above/behind _____.  → "The hold is below the deck."
```

**Maritime examples:**
- "The bridge is on the top of the superstructure."
- "The lifeboats are on the starboard side."
- "The engine room is at the stern."
- "The hold is below the deck."
- "The propeller is behind the hull."
- "The gangway is on the port side."

**Injected in:**
- Session 2 (Reading) — locating equipment in the PSC inspection
- Session 3 (Listening) — locating parts during the ship tour audio
- Session 4 (Grammar) — practice with prepositions of location
- Session 5 (Writing) — specifying locations in the checklist

---

### FUNCTION 4: IDENTIFYING

**What it is:** Saying what something is when you point to it or name it.

**Key structures:**
```
This + is + noun                      → "This is the bridge."
That + is + noun                      → "That is the engine room."
What + is + this/that?                → "What is this?" → "This is the radar."
What + do you call + this?            → "What do you call this?" → "It is a davit."
```

**Maritime examples:**
- "What is this?" — "This is the bridge."
- "What is that?" — "That is the funnel."
- "What do you call this?" — "It is a hatch."
- "What are these?" — "These are mooring lines."
- "What are those?" — "Those are life jackets."

**Injected in:**
- Session 2 (Reading) — identifying equipment in the inspection article
- Session 3 (Listening) — identifying parts during the audio tour
- Session 4 (Pronunciation) — Q&A drills for identification
- Session 5 (Speaking) — PSC inspector simulation

```

```

### Classifying chunks:
```

```


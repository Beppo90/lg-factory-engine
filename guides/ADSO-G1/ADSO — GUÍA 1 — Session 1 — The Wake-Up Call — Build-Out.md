## SET-UP DETALLADO (15 min) — 0:00 a 0:15

### Warm-up: "The Big Question" (10 min)

**Paso 1 — Pensar individualmente (3 min)**
1. Los aprendices entran al aula. El tablero ya muestra la pregunta: *"What is the most important part of a computer?"*
2. Instructor dice: *"Good morning everyone. Welcome to The Hardware Specialist. Look at the board. I want you to think — just think — about this question. No talking yet. You have 1 minute."*
3. Timer: 1 minuto de silencio. Instructor espera.

**Paso 2 — Compartir con vecino (2 min)**
4. *"Now turn to the person next to you. Share your answer. You have 2 minutes. Go."*
5. Timer: 2 minutos. Instructor circula, escucha las respuestas.

**Paso 3 — Recoger respuestas en plenaria (5 min)**
6. *"Ok, stop. Let's hear some answers. Who wants to share?"*
7. Recoger 5-6 respuestas. Escribir cada una en LIVE ZONE 1 del tablero.
8. No corregir ni evaluar — solo registrar. Ejemplos típicos: "CPU", "RAM", "the screen", "the brain", "the keyboard".

💡 *Si la clase está muy callada y nadie quiere compartir, diga: "Ok, I'll start. I think the most important part is... the power supply. Because without power, NOTHING works. Agree or disagree?" Esto rompe el hielo.*

### Teacher Talk — Opening Script (3 min)

*"Good morning everyone. My name is [nombre del instructor]. Welcome to Guía 1: The Hardware Specialist. For the next 8 sessions, you are going to learn the English of hardware — the parts inside your computer, how to describe them, how to request them, and how to talk about them with people from other countries. Today is Day 1. Before we start learning, I want to know — what do YOU already know? And what do you NOT know? Today is about discovery."*

### Objective (escrito en tablero)

*"Today you will: discover what you already know about hardware — and what you need to learn in this guide."*

**ICQ:**
- *"Are we starting with a test today?"* → Esperan: "No"
- *"What are we doing today?"* → Esperan: "Discovering what we know / finding out what we don't know"

### BLOQUE A — THE SPARK (70 min) — 0:15 a 1:25

**Worksheet:** PM-2.1 (The Spark)
**Agrupación:** Plenary → Individual → Groups of 3 → Individual
**Objetivo del bloque:** El aprendiz identifica sus propios vacíos de conocimiento y se motiva para llenarlos durante la guía.

---

#### Spark: Lectura del escenario (15 min) — 0:15 a 0:30

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. Proyectar slide 2 (imagen del escenario).
2. Instructor lee el escenario en voz alta, con dramatismo moderado:

*"It's Friday night. Your team has a big software project. The deadline is Monday. Suddenly, one computer in the office crashes. The screen goes black. There is smoke coming from the back of the machine. Nobody in the team knows what happened. The boss says: 'We need this computer working NOW. The project is inside.' But nobody can open the computer. Nobody knows the name of the parts inside. Nobody can call technical support because... the support manual is in English."*

3. Después de leer: *"Now — read it again, silently, by yourself. Underline any words you don't understand. You have 5 minutes."*
4. Timer: 5 minutos de lectura silenciosa.
5. *"Do you have any words you don't understand? Ask me."* — Responder 2-3 preguntas de vocabulario máximo. No traducir todo — solo las palabras críticas para entender la historia.

💡 *Los aprendices A1 pueden preguntar por "smoke", "deadline", "crashes". Defina con gestos o palabras simples: "Smoke = humo" (señalar), "Crash = when a computer dies suddenly" (gesto de apagón).*

---

#### Spark: Pregunta Polémica + Debate (25 min) — 0:30 a 0:55

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. Proyectar slide 3 con la Pregunta Polémica. Escribir también en el tablero:

**"If you don't know the NAMES of the parts inside a computer in English... can you really call yourself a software developer?"**

2. Instructor dice: *"This is the Controversial Question. There are NO wrong answers. I want your opinion. Form groups of 3 — now."*
3. Transición: 1 minuto para formar grupos.
4. *"Discuss this question in your group. Use these phrases"* (señalar las phrases del worksheet en slide 3):
   - *"I think... YES / NO because..."*
   - *"In my opinion..."*
   - *"I agree / I disagree because..."*
5. *"You have 10 minutes. At the end, your group writes ONE conclusion on your worksheet. Go."*
6. Timer: 10 minutos. Instructor circula por los grupos.
7. Durante la circulación: escuchar argumentos. Tomar nota mental de 2-3 grupos con conclusiones interesantes (para pedirles que compartan).

💡 *Mientras circula, NO entre en el debate como participante. Solo observe y tome nota de qué grupos tienen conclusiones claras. Si un grupo está en español, está bien — la actividad es de reflexión, no de producción lingüística.*

8. *"Ok, stop. Let's hear from some groups. Group 3 — what did you decide?"*
9. 3-4 grupos comparten su conclusión (1 minuto por grupo). Instructor escribe palabras clave de las respuestas en el tablero (no oraciones completas — solo ideas).
10. Después de escuchar: *"Interesting. Some of you said YES, some said NO. The truth is — this guide exists because this is a real problem. Developers work with international teams. The manuals are in English. The support lines are in English. By the end of this guide, you will be able to make that call."*

---

#### Spark: Activity 1 — Personal Connection (10 min) — 1:05 a 1:15

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Now — open your PM-2.1 worksheet. Look at Activity 1. You see 3 questions with a scale of 1 to 5. Answer them HONESTLY. This is for YOU, not for me. You have 3 minutes."*
2. Timer: 3 minutos. Individual.
3. *"Done? Now share with the person next to you. Compare your numbers. Are they similar or different? You have 3 minutes."*
4. Timer: 3 minutos. Pairs.
5. *"Anyone have a 5 on the first question? And a 1 on the third?"* — 2-3 voluntarios comparten brevemente.

**ICQ:**
- *"Are you writing sentences or just circling numbers?"* → Esperan: "Circling numbers"
- *"Do you share with the whole class or just your partner?"* → Esperan: "Just my partner"

---

#### Spark: Activity 3 — The "Need to Know" (10 min) — 1:15 a 1:25

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Look at Activity 3 on your worksheet. Imagine you are the person in the scenario. The computer crashed. You need to call technical support in the United States. What do you need to learn TODAY to make that call? Write 2 things."*
2. *"You have 5 minutes. In English or Spanish — whatever helps you think."*
3. Timer: 5 minutos. Individual.
4. *"Quick share — turn to your partner. Read your 2 things to each other. 2 minutes."*
5. Timer: 2 minutos. Pairs.

💡 *Esta actividad es metacognitiva — los aprendices no necesitan escribir en inglés perfecto. Si escriben en español, está bien. El objetivo es que IDENTIFIQUEN sus vacíos, no que los formulen perfectamente.*

✓ **Checkpoint:** *"Raise your hand if you wrote 'hardware vocabulary' as one of your needs."* — Contar manos. Si >70% lo escribió, confirma que la guía lo cubrirá.

**Transition → BREAK:**
*"Good work. You've identified what you need. Now — take a break. 15 minutes. Come back ready to find out exactly what your blind spots are."*

### BLOQUE B — THE GAP ANALYSIS (55 min) — 1:40 a 2:35

**Worksheet:** PM-2.2 (The Gap Analysis)
**Agrupación:** Plenary → Individual → Pairs → Individual
**Objetivo del bloque:** El aprendiz diagnostica sus saberes previos, identifica sus puntos ciegos y crea un contrato de aprendizaje personalizado.

---

#### Transición + Gap Analysis intro (5 min) — 1:40 a 1:45

**Teacher Talk — Transition:**
*"Ok, welcome back. In the Spark, we talked about the big picture — why hardware English matters. Now we get SPECIFIC. I'm going to show you the inside of a computer. You tell me: can you name the parts? Let's find out your blind spots."*

1. Proyectar slide 4 (imagen ASCII del interior del computador de PM-2.2).
2. *"Look at this image. Components A, B, C, D, E. Can you name them in English? Don't answer yet — just look. This is what we're going to work with."*

---

#### Gap Analysis: Activity 1 — Brainstorming (10 min) — 1:45 a 1:55

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Open your PM-2.2 worksheet. Activity 1 — 'What I Know'. Write ALL the computer words you already know in English. There is no minimum. Even 1 word is perfect. You have 5 minutes."*
2. Timer: 5 minutos. Individual. Silencio.
3. *"Done? Count your words. How many do you have? Tell your partner."* — 30 segundos.
4. *"Who has more than 5 words? More than 10?"* — Puños arriba. Celebrar los que tienen más.

💡 *Algunos aprendices se bloquean porque piensan que tienen que saber "mucho". Refuerce: "Even 1 word is a starting point. We go from there." Si alguien tiene 0 palabras, dígale en voz baja: "Do you know 'computer'? There — you have 1."*

---

#### Gap Analysis: Activity 2 — Blind Spots (15 min) — 1:55 a 2:10

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Now look at Activity 2 — 'What I Don't Know'. There are 5 situations. Can you say them in English? If YES — write it. If NO — leave it blank or put a ❓. Be honest. No one is grading this."*
2. *"You have 5 minutes. Go."*
3. Timer: 5 minutos. Individual. Silencio.

**Las 5 situaciones del worksheet:**
1. The name of the component that processes ALL information → CPU / Processor
2. The name of the TEMPORARY memory of a computer → RAM
3. How to say: "This computer TIENE 16 gigas de RAM" → "This computer has 16 gigabytes of RAM"
4. How to say: "The monitor IS a device for output" → "The monitor is an output device"
5. The difference between SSD and HDD in English → SSD is faster, HDD is mechanical

**Answer Key (para referencia del instructor — NO revelar a los aprendices todavía):**
Las respuestas correctas se revelan a lo largo de la guía. En esta sesión, el objetivo es que los aprendices IDENTIFIQUEN que no saben, no que aprendan las respuestas.

4. *"Now — turn to your partner. Compare your Blind Spots. Which ones are blank for BOTH of you? You have 5 minutes."*
5. Timer: 5 minutos. Pairs.
6. *"Ok. How many pairs have at least 3 blank spots?"* — Contar manos.
7. *"Good. These are your blind spots. And these are what we will fix in this guide."*

💡 *Si un par tiene TODAS las respuestas (lo cual es raro pero posible en aprendices con más nivel), dígales: "Great — you know a lot already. Your job in this guide is to help your classmates AND to go deeper. Can you explain the difference between SSD and HDD in 3 sentences? Write that."*

---

#### Gap Analysis: Activity 3 — Learning Contract (15 min) — 2:10 a 2:25

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Now — Activity 3. 'What I Need to Learn'. Based on your Blind Spots, write YOUR Learning Contract. Complete the sentence: 'In this guide, I need to learn:' — and write 3 or 4 things."*
2. *"This is YOUR contract with yourself. You will sign it and date it."*
3. *"You have 8 minutes. Go."*
4. Timer: 8 minutos. Individual.
5. *"Done? Read your contract to your partner. Do you have the same needs? You have 3 minutes."*
6. Timer: 3 minutos. Pairs.
7. *"Sign your contract. Date it. Keep it in your folder — you'll look at it again in Session 8."*

**ICQ:**
- *"How many things do you write in the contract?"* → Esperan: "3 or 4"
- *"Do you sign it?"* → Esperan: "Yes"
- *"When do you look at it again?"* → Esperan: "Session 8" (o "the last session")

💡 *El Learning Contract es un compromiso psicológico, no un documento legal. Refuerce la importancia: "This is a promise to yourself. When you finish this guide, you'll check — did I keep my promise?"*

## DIFFERENTIATION NOTES

### Fast Finishers

- **Activity 1 (Brainstorming):** Si termina con >10 palabras en 3 minutos: *"Can you put your words into categories? Input, Output, Storage, Internal. Write the category next to each word."*
- **Activity 2 (Blind Spots):** Si completa las 5 situaciones fácilmente: *"For each one you KNOW — write the full sentence, not just the word. Example: 'The component that processes all information is the CPU.'"*
- **Activity 3 (Learning Contract):** Si termina antes: *"Write 2 MORE things you want to learn that are NOT on the worksheet. Think about your future job as a developer."*
- **Peer tutor role:** *"Help your partner with Activity 2. Don't give the answer — ask questions to help them figure it out."*

### More Support Needed

- **Activity 1 (Brainstorming):** Si tiene 0-1 palabras después de 2 minutos: *"Open your phone. Search 'parts of a computer' in images. Look at the labels. Write 3 words you see. It's ok."*
- **Activity 2 (Blind Spots):** Permitir uso de diccionario (celular o físico) para las 5 situaciones. No es trampa — el objetivo es que IDENTIFIQUEN los vacíos, no que adivinen.
- **Activity 3 (Learning Contract):** Proporcionar sentence starter: *"In this guide, I need to learn: 1. The name of _________ in English. 2. How to say _________. 3. _________."*
- **Exit Ticket:** Si no puede escribir en inglés, aceptar español. El objetivo es la reflexión, no la producción.

### Para clases muy grandes (>25 aprendices)

- **Debate (Spark):** En lugar de 1 plenaria, usar 2 rondas: grupos de 3 comparten, luego cada grupo de 3 se junta con otro grupo de 3 para una discusión de 6 personas. Después 2-3 grupos grandes comparten con la plenaria.
- **Blind Spots comparison:** En lugar de parejas, usar grupos de 4. Es más rápido y genera más datos para la síntesis colectiva.

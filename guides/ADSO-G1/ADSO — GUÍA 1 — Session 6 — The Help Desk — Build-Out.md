## SET-UP (20 min)

### Warm-up: Chunk Recall (8 min)

**Procedimiento:**
1. Instructor writes on the board: *"What speaking chunks did we learn in Session 3?"*
2. Students recall individually (1 min), then share with partner (2 min)
3. Collect on board: Greet, Describe Problem, Confirm Solution
4. Quick round: Instructor says the function, ALL students say the phrase aloud together (choral drill, 2 min)

**Teacher Talk:** *"These are your weapons for today. You'll need all of them."*

### Objective & Mission Brief (12 min)

**Procedimiento:**
1. Distribute PM-3.5: Mission Brief (1 por estudiante)
2. Read the Mission Brief together as a class:
   - **The Situation:** "Your company, DevCore Solutions, needs a new workstation for a new developer. You are the Hardware Specialist team. Your task: (1) DISCUSS with your team to decide the specs, (2) PRODUCE a Technical Spec Sheet document, (3) PRESENT your Spec Sheet to the class."
   - **Budget:** $1,200 USD
   - **Requirements:** The developer needs a workstation for: coding, running virtual machines, video calls, occasional video editing
3. Distribute PM-2.8: Cue Cards — review the 5 phases together (Opening → Problem → Details → Solution → Closing)
4. Assign roles in teams of 3:
   - **Team Lead:** Leads the discussion, presents orally to the class
   - **Recorder:** Writes the Spec Sheet document
   - **Auditor:** Checks accuracy of specs, grammar, and vocabulary

**Teacher Talk:** *"This is NOT a test. This is your Final Mission. You speak, you decide, you write, you present. English first — but if you get stuck, ask your team in Spanish and then try again in English. The goal: your team UNDERSTANDS each other, the problem GETS SOLVED, and you LEAVE with a real document."*

**Reglas del Mission:**
1. All discussion in English (Spanish only if completely stuck)
2. Team must AGREE on all specs before writing
3. Spec Sheet must have: 5+ components, specs for each, category, justification
4. Each team presents to the class (2-3 min)

**Agrupación:** Plenary → Teams of 3 (assigned by instructor)

## WHILE (145 min)

> **Timeline note:** The 4 explicit blocks total 125 min (50+15+45+15). The remaining 20 min are absorbed by: team formation and role assignment (5 min), material distribution between blocks (5 min), natural transitions and time-keeping (5 min), and buffer for classes that need more discussion time (5 min). In practice, these 20 min are not "empty" — they're operational overhead that varies by class size.

### Bloque A: Team Discussion (50 min)

**Procedimiento:**
1. Teams open their Cue Cards (PM-2.8, face-up)
2. Teams DISCUSS the workstation requirements using the Cue Card phases:
   - Opening: "OK team, we need to build a workstation..."
   - Problem: "The developer needs to run VMs and video editing..."
   - Details: "I think we need [component] because..."
   - Solution: "Let's go with [X] and [Y]..."
   - Closing: "So our final decision is..."
3. Teams must DECIDE:
   - CPU: what type, what speed?
   - RAM: how much?
   - Storage: SSD or HDD or both? How much?
   - GPU: needed or not? Which one?
   - Other: monitor, keyboard, mouse, ports?
4. Teams outline their decisions on paper (NOT the Spec Sheet yet — just notes)
5. Instructor circulates with Observation Checklist, listening for:
   - Use of hardware vocabulary (CPU, RAM, SSD, etc.)
   - Use of grammar structures (is/has/this/that)
   - Intelligibility (can the team understand each other?)
   - Decision-making (do they reach a conclusion?)

**Timing:** 40 min discussion + 10 min to outline decisions

**Agrupación:** Teams of 3

**Teacher Talk during circulation:** *"Good use of 'compatible'. Remember: 'It has 16 GB of RAM' not 'It have'."*

**Common errors to listen for:**
- "It have" → "It has"
- "The CPU are" → "The CPU is"
- Missing articles: "I need SSD" → "I need an SSD"
- Wrong comparatives: "more faster" → "faster"

---

### Bloque B: Break / Transition (15 min)

**Teacher Talk:** *"Good discussions. I heard some great arguments about SSD vs HDD. Now — your decisions need to become a DOCUMENT. The Spec Sheet is your PRODUCT. Every team needs to produce one."*

---

### Bloque C: Spec Sheet Production (45 min)

**Procedimiento:**
1. Distribute PM-3.5: Spec Sheet Template (1 por equipo)
2. The Recorder writes while Team Lead and Auditor review
3. Spec Sheet format:

```
TECHNICAL SPEC SHEET
Company: DevCore Solutions
Workstation for: New Developer
Budget: $1,200 USD

| # | Component | Specification | Category | Justification |
|---|-----------|--------------|----------|---------------|
| 1 |           |              |          |               |
| 2 |           |              |          |               |
| 3 |           |              |          |               |
| 4 |           |              |          |               |
| 5 |           |              |          |               |

TOTAL ESTIMATED COST: $_____

Approved by: [Team Lead name]
```

4. Requirements for each row:
   - **Component:** Name in English (CPU, RAM, SSD, etc.)
   - **Specification:** Speed, capacity, type (e.g., "Intel Core i5, 3.4 GHz")
   - **Category:** Input / Output / Storage / Internal
   - **Justification:** 1 sentence in English explaining WHY this component (must use "is", "has", or "needs")

5. Auditor checks: vocabulary correct? Grammar correct? Specs make sense? Budget adds up?

**Timing:** 35 min production + 10 min review/revision

**Agrupación:** Teams of 3

**Instructor circulates:** Helps teams with vocabulary, checks grammar, verifies technical accuracy.

---

### Bloque D: Oral Presentation (15 min)

**Procedimiento:**
1. Each Team Lead presents their Spec Sheet to the class (2-3 min per team)
2. Format of presentation:
   - "Good morning. We are Team [X]. Our workstation is for a new developer."
   - "We need [component 1] because..."
   - "We chose [component 2] because..."
   - "Our total cost is $[X]."
3. After each presentation: 1 question from another team
4. Instructor evaluates with Observation Checklist

**If time is short (large class):** Present only 3-4 teams. Other teams submit Spec Sheets for review.

**Agrupación:** Plenary (presentation)

**Teacher Talk:** *"Listen carefully to each team. After each presentation, you can ask ONE question. Be respectful — this is their Final Mission."*

## WRAP-UP (15 min)

### Exit Ticket: Communication Deliverable Checklist (5 min)

Students self-assess (individual):
- ✓ Team discussed ALL components needed for the workstation
- ✓ Spec Sheet has 5+ items with specifications, category, and justification
- ✓ My presentation was intelligible (my classmates understood me)
- ✓ I used grammar correctly (is/has/this/that) at least 5 times
- ✓ I used at least 8 hardware vocabulary words

### Teacher Talk (5 min)

*"You did it. This was your Final Mission. You had real conversations in English, made real decisions as a team, and produced a real document. You presented to the class. That's what a Hardware Specialist does. Next session — the quiz. Prove what you know."*

### Trabajo Autónomo (5 min)

Workbook Ch. 6 — Simulation Prep:
- Write a reflection paragraph (5 sentences): *"What was easy in the Final Mission? What was hard? What would you do differently?"*
- Review ALL vocabulary and grammar for the quiz next session
- Time estimado: 45 min

## CÓMO USAR ESTE BUILD-OUT

1. **Antes de la sesión:** Imprime todos los materiales. Corta nada — no hay tarjetas que cortar en esta versión. Prepara el tablero según el Board Plan.

2. **Durante la sesión:** Sigue el SET-UP → WHILE → WRAP-UP. Circula constantemente durante Bloque A y C. Usa el Observation Checklist. No corrijas en voz alta durante la discusión — anota y corrige después.

3. **Después de la sesión:** Revisa los Observation Checklists. Los resultados alimentan el Feedback Loop (Session 8) y la evaluación de PM-4.1.

4. **Coherencia con PM-3.5:** Este Build-Out implementa el arquetipo "Professional Simulation" del PM-3.5: el equipo simula ser un Hardware Specialist team, produce un Spec Sheet (producto), y lo presenta oralmente (desempeño). Las dos evidencias SENA se producen en esta sesión.

### TASK 0B: Review the Formula

*Look at the demonstrative structures. You will hear these in the audio.*

```
THIS + IS  →  "This is the bridge."         (singular, near)
THAT + IS  →  "That is the engine room."    (singular, far)
THESE + ARE → "These are lifeboats."         (plural, near)
THOSE + ARE → "Those are mooring lines."     (plural, far)
```

## 🎧 AUDIO SCRIPT — TEACHER REFERENCE

*This is the script for TTS generation. Teacher reads or generates audio at 0.85x speed.*

---

**THE SHIP TOUR — Chief Officer Vargas Shows the Vessel**

*[Chief Officer Ana Lucía Vargas speaks slowly and clearly]*

"Welcome aboard MV Caribbean Star. My name is Chief Officer Ana Lucía Vargas. Today I am going to show you the main parts of this vessel. Please follow me.

First — look up. **This is the bridge.** The bridge is the command station of the ship. Here, the officers navigate. **That is the radar** on the console. And **this is the compass** — the magnetic compass.

Now — look at the top of the ship. **This is the superstructure.** The bridge is on the superstructure. And **that is the funnel** — where the engine exhaust goes out.

Now we go down to the deck. **This is the main deck.** The deck is the working surface of the ship. Look — **these are the hatches.** The hatches are the openings on the deck. Under the hatches, **that is the hold.** The hold is where we carry the cargo.

Now look to the left. **This is the port side.** And on the port side — **this is a lifeboat.** One lifeboat on the port side, one on the starboard side. **These are the life jackets** — near the lifeboats. **Those are the fire extinguishers** — on the wall.

Now look to the right. **This is the starboard side.** And **that is the gangway.** The gangway is how people board the ship from the quay.

Now look at the front of the ship. **That is the bow.** And at the bow — **those are the mooring lines.** We use the mooring lines to tie the ship to the dock.

Now look at the back. **That is the stern.** And under the water — **that is the propeller.** The propeller turns and moves the ship.

And below the deck — **that is the engine room.** The engine room is the heart of the ship. The main engine is there.

That is the end of our tour. Do you have any questions?"

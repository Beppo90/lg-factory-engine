## SET-UP DETALLADO (15 min) — 0:00 a 0:15

### Warm-up: "Vocabulary Lightning Round" (10 min)

**Paso 1 — Rapid naming (5 min)**

1. Los aprendices entran. Proyectar slide 15 (imágenes de componentes hardware).
2. Instructor dice:
*"Good morning everyone. Look at the screen. I point — you say the word. FAST. Ready?"*
3. Instructor señala cada imagen, una por una. Los aprendices dicen el nombre en coro. 20 imágenes, ritmo rápido (1 cada 3-4 segundos).
4. Si la clase no responde en 3 segundos, instructor dice la palabra y la clase repite.

**Paso 2 — Identify hard words (5 min)**

5. *"Good. Now — which words were HARD to pronounce? Tell me."*

7. *"Today we fix that. We learn HOW to say these words correctly. And then — we learn HOW to build sentences with the grammar."*

💡 *Los aprendices hispanohablantes típicamente tienen dificultad con: la /ð/ de "motherboard", la schwa /ə/ en sílabas átonas, y el estrés de palabras como "compatible" (que en español tiene estrés diferente). No corrija en el warm-up — solo identifique.*

Grammar — how to build sentences with 'is,' 'has,' and 'this/that/these/those.' By the end of today, you can describe any computer in English. Ready?"*

#### Phonetic Toolbox: Introducción (5 min) — 0:15 a 0:20

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. Proyectar slide 16 (Phonetic Toolbox con las 10 palabras).
2. Instructor presenta las 10 palabras, una por una. Para CADA una:

   - Dice la guía fonética: *"/pro-CE-sor/ — stress on the second syllable."*
   - Marca la sílaba acentuada con voz más fuerte: *"pro-CE-sor"*
   - Los aprendices repiten 2 veces en coro.

3. Las 10 palabras en orden:
   1. **Processor** — /pro-**CE**-sor/ — 2nd syllable
   2. **Compatible** — /com-**PA**-ti-bol/ — 2nd syllable
   3. **Monitor** — /**MÓ**-ni-tor/ — 1st syllable
   4. **Keyboard** — /**QUÍI**-bord/ — 1st syllable
   5. **Hardware** — /**JÁRD**-uer/ — 1st syllable
   6. **Software** — /**SÓFT**-uer/ — 1st syllable
   7. **Upgrade** — /**ÓP**-greid/ — 1st syllable
   8. **Gigabyte** — /**GÍ**-ga-bait/ — 1st syllable
   9. **Ethernet** — /**Í**-zer-net/ — 1st syllable

💡 *"HDMI" es una excepción — se deletrea, no se lee como palabra. Los aprendices suelen intentar pronunciarlo como "jádmi." Corrija: "It's H-D-M-I — four letters. The stress is on the last letter: ÁI."*

#### Activity 2 — Chunking Drill (15 min) — 0:35 a 0:50

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Activity 2. The Chunking Drill. 5 chunks — word groups that native speakers say FAST, as one unit. Your job: say them fast, WITHOUT pauses between words."*
2. Instructor presenta cada chunk, uno por uno:
   - Primero dice el chunk LENTO: *"The — CPU — is"* (con pausas)
   - Luego dice el chunk RÁPIDO: *"The CPU is"* (todo junto)
   - Los aprendices repiten rápido 3 veces.

3. Los 5 chunks:
   1. **"The CPU is"** — /de-sii-pii-yú-IS/ — todo junto
   2. **"It has a"** — /it-JÁ-sa/ — suena como una sola palabra
   3. **"This is an"** /DÍ-si-san/ — todo junto
   4. **"faster than"** /FÁS-ter-dan/ — sin pausa
   5. **"compatible with"** /com-PA-ti-bol-UID/ — todo junto

4. Después de practicar los 5: *"Now — pair up. Take turns: one person says the chunk, the other repeats. 3 times each chunk. 5 minutes."*
5. Timer: 5 minutos. Pairs.

6. *"Who can say all 5 fast? Volunteer?"* — 1-2 voluntarios dicen los 5 chunks. Instructor da feedback positivo.

💡 *El chunk "It has a" es el más difícil porque "has" casi desaparece a velocidad natural. Los aprendices suelen decir "It... has... a" con pausas. Dígales: "In real English, 'It has a' sounds like ONE word: 'it-JÁ-sa.' Practice that."*

#### Transición + Syntax Blueprint (10 min) — 1:20 a 1:30

**Teacher Talk — Transition:**
*"Welcome back. You can say the words. Now — let's build SENTENCES. Three structures. Everything you need to describe hardware. Look at the board."*

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. Instructor presenta las 3 estructuras, una por una. Para CADA una:
   - Escribe la fórmula en el tablero (Grammar Formulas zone)
   - Da 2 ejemplos en voz alta
   - Los aprendices copian la fórmula y los ejemplos en su PM-2.7 worksheet (Syntax Blueprint section)

**Structure 1: VERB TO BE (Definitions)**
*"Formula: [Component] + IS + [definition or adjective]. Example: 'The CPU is the brain of the computer.' 'The monitor is small.' Write it."*
- Timer: 1 minuto para copiar.

**Structure 2: HAVE / HAS (Possession & Specs)**
*"Formula: [Device] + HAS + [feature or specification]. Example: 'The laptop has a mechanical keyboard.' 'This computer has 16 GB of RAM.' Write it."*
- Timer: 1 minuto para copiar.

**Structure 3: DEMONSTRATIVES (Identifying)**
*"Formula: THIS/THAT + IS + [singular]. 'This is an HDMI port.' THESE/THOSE + ARE + [plural]. 'Those are USB cables.' Write it."*
- Timer: 1 minuto para copiar.

2. *"Quick — all three formulas. Read them to your partner. 1 minute."*
3. Timer: 1 minuto. Pairs.

💡 *Los aprendices A1 suelen confundir "is" y "has" — usan "is" donde necesitan "has" y viceversa. Refuerce la diferencia: "IS = what it IS (definition, adjective). HAS = what it CONTAINS or HAS (feature, spec, part)."*

#### Activity 1 — Syntax Bugs (20 min) — 1:30 a 1:50

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Activity 1. Syntax Bugs. 6 sentences. Some are CORRECT ✓, some have a BUG ✗. Find the bug and select the correct version. You have 5 minutes."*
2. Timer: 5 minutos. Individual.

**Las 6 sentences del worksheet:**

| # | Sentence | ✓ or ✗ | If ✗, correct version |
|---|----------|--------|-----------------------|
| 1 | "The RAM are 16 gigabytes." | ✗ | "The RAM **is** 16 gigabytes." |
| 2 | "The CPU is the processor." | ✓ | — |
| 3 | "This laptop have an SSD." | ✗ | "This laptop **has** an SSD." |
| 4 | "Those is USB cables." | ✗ | "Those **are** USB cables." |
| 5 | "The monitor has an HDMI port." | ✓ | — |
| 6 | "An SSD is more faster than an HDD." | ✗ | "An SSD is **faster** than an HDD." (no "more") |

3. *"Compare with your partner. Same bugs? Discuss. 3 minutes."*
4. Timer: 3 minutos. Pairs.
5. *"Let's check. Number 1 — correct or bug?"* → "Bug — should be 'is' not 'are'"
6. *"Why?"* → "RAM is singular — it's one thing."
7. Revisar los 6 ítems. Para cada bug, instructor:
   - Escribe la versión incorrecta en LIVE ZONE con ✗ en rojo
   - Escribe la versión correcta debajo con ✓ en verde
   - Pregunta: *"Why is it wrong? What's the rule?"*

**Answer Key (in-line):**

| # | Verdict | Explanation |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | ✗ | "RAM" is singular → "is" not "are" |
| 2 | ✓ | — |
| 3 | ✗ | "This laptop" is singular → "has" not "have" |
| 4 | ✗ | "Those" is plural → "are" not "is" |
| 5 | ✓ | — |
| 6 | ✗ | "Faster" is already comparative → no "more" |

💡 *El ítem 6 ("more faster") es un error muy común con aprendices hispanohablantes porque en español se dice "más rápido." Explique: "In English, 'faster' already HAS the 'more' inside. You don't say 'more faster.' Just 'faster.'"*

#### Activity 2 — Fill the Ticket (20 min) — 1:50 a 2:10

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Activity 2. Fill the Ticket. A real Support Ticket with blanks. Fill in IS, HAS, THIS, THAT, THESE, or THOSE. You have 7 minutes."*
2. Timer: 7 minutos. Individual.

**El Support Ticket (para referencia):**

> **SUPPORT TICKET #0042**
>
> **Problem:** The workstation _______ very slow. The CPU _______ an old i3 processor. _______ processor _______ not compatible with the new software.
>
> **Details:** The computer _______ only 4 GB of RAM. _______ _______ not enough for development. The storage _______ an HDD. _______ _______ slow drives.
>
> **Additional:** _______ _______ the keyboard (pointing to desk). It _______ OK. But _______ cables over there — _______ _______ old USB 2.0 cables. We need USB 3.0.

**Answer Key (in-line):**

| Blank | Answer | Rule |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1. "The workstation _______ very slow" | **is** | To Be — describing |
| 2. "The CPU _______ an old i3" | **is** | To Be — defining |
| 3. "_______ processor" | **This** | Demonstrative — singular, near |
| 4. "_______ not compatible" | **is** | To Be — adjective |
| 5. "The computer _______ only 4 GB" | **has** | Have/Has — specs |
| 6. "_______ _______ not enough" | **This is** | Demonstrative + To Be |
| 7. "The storage _______ an HDD" | **is** | To Be — defining |
| 8. "_______ _______ slow drives" | **Those are** | Demonstrative — plural, far |
| 9. "_______ _______ the keyboard" | **This is** | Demonstrative — pointing, near |
| 10. "It _______ OK" | **is** | To Be — adjective |
| 11. "_______ cables over there" | **Those** | Demonstrative — plural, far |
| 12. "_______ _______ old USB 2.0" | **Those are** | Demonstrative + To Be |

3. *"Compare with your partner. 3 minutes."*
4. Timer: 3 minutos. Pairs.
5. *"Let's check — blank by blank."* — Revisar los 12 blanks en plenaria. Instructor escribe respuestas en LIVE ZONE.

💡 *Los blanks 3, 6, 8, 9, 11 y 12 requieren demonstratives. Los aprendices suelen tener más dificultad con THESE/THOSE (plural) que con THIS/THAT. Refuerce: "THIS/THAT = one thing. THESE/THOSE = more than one thing. And THIS/THESE = near you. THAT/THOSE = far from you."*

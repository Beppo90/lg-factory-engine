### YOUR GRAMMAR RULES — WRITE THEM DOWN

*Based on your discoveries, write the complete rules.*

**DEMONSTRATIVES:**

```
SINGULAR (one thing):
  NEAR → __________ + is + noun     "__________ is the bridge."
  FAR  → __________ + is + noun     "__________ is the engine room."

PLURAL (more than one thing):
  NEAR → __________ + are + noun    "__________ are lifeboats."
  FAR  → __________ + are + noun    "__________ are mooring lines."
```

**VERB TO BE:**

```
AFFIRMATIVE:
  Subject + __________ (singular)    "The hull __________ steel."
  Subject + __________ (plural)      "The lifeboats __________ on deck."

NEGATIVE:
  Subject + __________ / __________  "The hull __________ aluminum."
                                     "The lifeboats __________ on port."

QUESTION:
  __________ + Subject + complement? "__________ this the bridge?"
                                     "__________ the lifeboats on deck?"

SHORT ANSWER:
  Yes, it __________. / No, it __________.
  Yes, they __________. / No, they __________.
```

## PHASE 3 — C: COMMUNICATIVE GRAMMAR (15 min — Pairs)

### THE MYSTERY SHIP GAME

> One student describes a MYSTERY SHIP using ONLY demonstratives and verb to be. The other student guesses what type of ship it is by asking questions.

---

### RULES:
1. **Describer:** You have a mystery ship card. Describe it WITHOUT saying the type.
2. **Guesser:** Ask yes/no questions starting with "Is/Are..."
3. **Describer:** Answer only "Yes, it is/they are" or "No, it isn't/they aren't"
4. **Guesser:** Make a guess: "This is a _____ ship."

---

### MYSTERY SHIP CARDS

## TEACHER ANSWER KEY

### Phase 1 — Discovery Answers:

1. **B** — "This" = near, "That" = far
2. **A** — "These" = near, "Those" = far
3. is / are (singular vs plural)
4. are / are
5. **A** — "is" for singular, "are" for plural
6. **B** — Subject + is not / are not
7. **B** — Move "is/are" to the beginning

### Phase 2 — Error Analysis:

| # | Error | Correct |
|---|-------|---------|
| 1 | "This **are**" | "This **is** the bridge." |
| 2 | "That **are**" | "That **is** the engine room." |
| 3 | "These **is**" | "These **are** lifeboats." |
| 4a | "Those **is**" | "Those **are** the fire extinguishers." |
| 4b | "They **is**" | "They **are** on the wall." |
| 5 | "The hull **are**" | "The hull **is** steel." |
| 6a | "The stern **are**" | "The stern **is** at the back." |
| 7 | "Yes, **they** are" | "Yes, **it** is." (propeller = singular) |
| 8 | "That **are**" | "That **is** the hold below." |

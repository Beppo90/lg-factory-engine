### SESSION 1: THE WAKE-UP CALL

**Worksheets:** PM-2.1 (The Spark) + PM-2.2 (The Gap Analysis)
**Duración:** 180 minutos
**Habilidades foco:** — | **Soporte:** —

**SET-UP (15 min)**
- Warm-up: No previous session — this is the first. Instructor writes on the board: *"What is the most important part of a computer?"* Students think individually (1 min), then share with the person next to them (2 min). Collect 5-6 answers on the board.
- Objective: "Today you will discover what you already know about hardware — and what you need to learn in this guide."
- Teacher Talk: "Good morning everyone. Welcome to The Hardware Specialist. Before we start learning, I want to know — what do YOU already know? Today is about discovery."

**WHILE (150 min)**

- Bloque A (60 min): The Spark (PM-2.1) — Read the scenario aloud (developer, Friday night, crashed PC, deadline Monday). Students read individually. Controversial Question debate in groups of 3: *"If you don't know the names of the parts inside a computer in English... can you really call yourself a software developer?"* Each group shares conclusion with plenary. Activity 1: Personal Connection (rate 1-5). Activity 3: "Need to Know" — write 2 things they need to learn. Agrupación: Individual → Groups of 3 → Plenary.

- Bloque B (15 min): Break / Transition. Teacher Talk: "Ok, great debate. Now let's find out what you ACTUALLY know — and what your blind spots are."

- Bloque C (60 min): The Gap Analysis (PM-2.2) — Activity 1: Brainstorming (write all English computer words). Activity 2: Blind Spots (5 situations — can you say them in English?). Activity 3: Learning Contract (based on blind spots, write what they need to learn). Students sign the contract. Agrupación: Individual → Pairs (compare blind spots) → Plenary (share 1 blind spot each).

- Bloque D (15 min): Synthesis — Instructor collects the most common blind spots on the board. These become the "targets" for the guide. Teacher Talk: "Look at the board. These are YOUR blind spots. By the end of this guide, you will be able to do ALL of these things."

**WRAP-UP (15 min)**
- Exit Ticket: "Write ONE thing you are sure you know about hardware in English, and ONE thing you definitely don't know."
- Teacher Talk: "Before we go — one thing you know, one thing you don't. Next session we read a real tech request from a developer."
- Trabajo Autónomo: Workbook Ch. 1 — My Hardware Profile: draw your real computer at home or SENA, label 5 parts in English (even if wrong), and write 1 sentence about each. (45 min estimado)

### SESSION 2: READ THE REQUEST

**Worksheets:** PM-2.3 (The Master Anchor — Reading)
**Duración:** 180 minutos
**Habilidades foco:** R● | **Soporte:** V○

**SET-UP (15 min)**
- Warm-up: "Quick Recall" — Students share with a partner: *"What did you write in your Learning Contract last session? What do you need to learn?"* (3 min). Then 3 volunteers share with plenary.
- Objective: "Today you will read a REAL tech request email from a developer. You will extract information, make decisions, and learn the structures you need to write your OWN request."
- Teacher Talk: "Good morning everyone. Last session we discovered your blind spots. Today we read a real email from Carlos — a developer who has a problem with his workstation. Your job: understand the problem and decide what to do."

**WHILE (150 min)**

- Bloque A (20 min): Pre-Reading — Toolbelt (5 key words: Spec Sheet, Upgrade, Compatible, Performance, Budget). Instructor introduces each word with a simple example. Students write them in their worksheet. Quick check: instructor says a word, students say the meaning. Agrupación: Plenary.

- Bloque B (60 min): While-Reading — First read: students read the email silently (5 min). Gist question: *"What does Carlos want?"* (1 min pair discussion). Second read: Activity 1 — Information Transfer table (Current Spec vs. Requested Spec). Students work individually, then check in pairs. Instructor reviews with plenary. Agrupación: Individual → Pairs → Plenary.

- Bloque C (15 min): Break / Transition. Teacher Talk: "You understood what Carlos needs. Now YOU are the IT Manager — and you don't have enough money."

- Bloque D (40 min): Post-Reading — Activity 2: Technical Decision. Students are IT Managers with only $800 (not $1,200). Which TWO upgrades are most important? Circle choices, justify with a sentence from the text. Groups of 4 compare decisions and debate. Each group presents their choice. Agrupación: Individual → Groups of 4 → Plenary.

- Bloque E (15 min): Activity 3: Deconstruct for Future Output. Students find the EXACT sentences Carlos used to: describe a problem, request something, compare two things. These become their "writing formulas" for Session 5. Teacher Talk: "Save these sentences. You will use this SAME structure to write your own Tech Request later."

**WRAP-UP (15 min)**
- Exit Ticket: "Write Carlos's problem in YOUR words — 2 sentences using 'is' and 'has'."
- Teacher Talk: "Before we go — summarize Carlos's problem in your own words. Next session we LISTEN to Carlos on the phone."
- Trabajo Autónomo: Workbook Ch. 2 — Reading Extension: re-read the email and underline ALL examples of "is" and "has" (count them). Write 3 new sentences about your own computer using the same structure. (45 min estimado)

### SESSION 3: TUNING IN

**Worksheets:** PM-2.4 (The Auditory Anchor — Listening) + PM-2.5 (Vocabulary & Language Function)
**Duración:** 180 minutos
**Habilidades foco:** L● V● | **Soporte:** R○

**SET-UP (20 min)**
- Warm-up: "Quick Recall" — In pairs, each student names 3 hardware components they remember from Carlos's email (reciclaje PM-2.3). Then: *"Can you say them in a sentence with 'is' or 'has'?"* Quick round — 6 volunteers share 1 sentence each.
- Objective: "Today you will LISTEN to Carlos on the phone calling IT Support — and learn the 20 key vocabulary words you need for hardware."
- Teacher Talk: "Good morning everyone. Last session we READ Carlos's email. Today we're going to LISTEN to Carlos on the phone — and learn the exact words you need for hardware."

**WHILE (145 min)**

- Bloque A (50 min): Listening (PM-2.4) — Pre-listening: Audio Cues (explain "gig" = Gigabyte, "lemme check" = let me check, is/has sound similar at speed). First listen (gist): *"Who is calling? What is the problem?"* — individual, then pair check. Second listen (detail): Activity 1 — The Triage (match problem with component). Third listen (task completion): Activity 2 — The Immediate Action (which action first? justify). Activity 3: Noticing & Chunk Extraction — find exact phrases for: greet, describe problem, confirm solution. These become the "speaking blueprint" for Session 6. Agrupación: Individual → Pairs for checking → Plenary review.

- Bloque B (15 min): Break / Transition. Teacher Talk: "Great job with the listening. Now we're going to focus on the vocabulary — the words Carlos used on the phone."

- Bloque C (60 min): Vocabulary (PM-2.5) — Toolbelt visual categorization: display the 8 key chunks on the board, students copy. Activity 1: Logical Association (match component with function — 5 items). Activity 2: Gap Fill — The System Log (7 blanks, word bank with 7 words). Activity 3: Micro-Production — describe your own workstation at SENA using 2 sentences with Toolbelt words. Agrupación: Individual → Small groups (share sentences) → Plenary.

- Bloque D (20 min): Reciclaje — Return to the Reading text (PM-2.3). Students underline the 20 key terms wherever they appear. Compare: which words appeared in the email AND in the listening? Create a mini-concordance on the board.

**WRAP-UP (15 min)**
- Exit Ticket: "Write 3 hardware components and 1 sentence about each using 'has' or 'is'."
- Teacher Talk: "Before we go — write down three components and one sentence for each. Next session we work on pronunciation and grammar."
- Trabajo Autónomo: Workbook Ch. 3 — Vocabulary Reinforcement: categorization (Input/Output/Storage/Internal) + crossword + "My Ideal Workstation" paragraph (5 sentences). (45 min estimado)

### SESSION 4: SAY IT RIGHT, BUILD IT RIGHT

**Worksheets:** PM-2.6 (Pronunciation & Speaking Skills) + PM-2.7 (Grammar & Structure Use)
**Duración:** 180 minutos
**Habilidades foco:** P● G● | **Soporte:** V○

**SET-UP (15 min)**
- Warm-up: "Vocabulary Lightning Round" — Instructor points at images/components on Canva slides, students say the word aloud. 20 words, fast pace. Then: *"Which words are HARD to pronounce?"* Collect 4-5 difficult words on the board.
- Objective: "Today you will learn HOW to pronounce the hardware words correctly AND how to build sentences with the grammar you need."
- Teacher Talk: "Good morning everyone. You know the words. Now let's make sure you can SAY them and USE them in real sentences. Today is about accuracy."

**WHILE (150 min)**

- Bloque A (50 min): Pronunciation (PM-2.6) — Phonetic Toolbox: display 10 words with phonetic guide + stress marks. Activity 1: Perception — count syllables and circle stressed syllable (6 words). Activity 2: Chunking Drill — 5 chunks ("The CPU is", "It has a", "This is an", "faster than", "compatible with"). Say each 3 times fast. Instructor models, students repeat, pairs practice. Activity 3: The Voice Command — read the IT report aloud to a partner. Partner checks intelligibility (YES/PARTIALLY/NO). Swap roles. Agrupación: Plenary (modeling) → Individual → Pairs.

- Bloque B (15 min): Break / Transition. Teacher Talk: "Now you can say the words. Let's build the sentences. This is the grammar you need."

- Bloque C (60 min): Grammar (PM-2.7) — Syntax Blueprint: display 3 structures (To Be, Have/Has, Demonstratives) with formulas on the board. Activity 1: Syntax Bugs — 6 sentences, some correct ✓, some with a bug ✗. Find the bug, select correct version. Individual → pair check → plenary review. Activity 2: Fill the Ticket — Support Ticket #0042 (fill with IS, HAS, THIS, THAT, THESE, THOSE). Activity 3: Applied Production — write 3 sentences about a real SENA workstation using IS, HAS, and one demonstrative. Agrupación: Individual → Pairs → Plenary.

- Bloque D (20 min): Integration — Pronunciation + Grammar combined drill. Students read their 3 sentences from Activity 3 ALOUD to a partner. Partner checks: (1) Is the grammar correct? (2) Can I understand the pronunciation? Quick feedback round.

**WRAP-UP (15 min)**
- Exit Ticket: "Write one sentence using 'is', one using 'has', and one using 'this/that/these/those' — all about hardware."
- Teacher Talk: "Before we go — three sentences, three structures. Next session you WRITE your own Tech Request."
- Trabajo Autónomo: Workbook Ch. 4 — Grammar Drill: 10 fill-in-the-blank sentences (is/has/demonstratives) + 5 "Syntax Bug" corrections from scratch. (45 min estimado)

### SESSION 5: WRITE IT RIGHT

**Worksheets:** PM-2.8 (Writing Skills & Pragmatics)
**Duración:** 180 minutos
**Habilidades foco:** W● | **Soporte:** G○ V○ R○

**SET-UP (15 min)**
- Warm-up: "Formula Recall" — On the board, students reconstruct from memory the 3 grammar structures (To Be, Have/Has, Demonstratives). 3 volunteers write them. Then: *"Which sentences from Carlos's email used these structures?"* Quick recall of the "writing formulas" from Session 2.
- Objective: "Today you will WRITE your own Tech Request email. By the end of this session, you will have a complete, professional email in English."
- Teacher Talk: "Good morning everyone. You've read Carlos's email. You've listened to his phone call. You've learned the vocabulary, pronunciation, and grammar. TODAY — you write your own. This is where everything comes together."

**WHILE (150 min)**

- Bloque A (30 min): Deconstruct (PM-2.8, Activity 1) — Blueprint Model: display Carlos's simplified email on screen. Activity 1: Pragmatic Analysis — match each part of the email with its purpose (5 items: greeting, introduce problem, describe problem, request, close). Discuss: *"Why does the email start with the problem, not with 'How are you'?"* — because it's a TECH REQUEST, not a social email. Genre conventions matter. Agrupación: Individual → Pairs → Plenary.

- Bloque B (15 min): Break / Transition. Teacher Talk: "Now you know the structure. Let's practice filling it in before you write from scratch."

- Bloque C (45 min): Guided Drafting (PM-2.8, Activity 2) — Fill the Skeleton: a different developer has a problem (monitor is old, only VGA, mouse broken). Students complete the email using the Language Bank (has, is, need, not compatible, is old, This is). Instructor reviews 2-3 examples with plenary — focus on grammar accuracy and format. Agrupación: Individual → Plenary (review).

- Bloque D (45 min): The Final Task (PM-2.8, Activity 3) — Write YOUR OWN Tech Request for a workstation at SENA. Students think about a REAL computer they use. They write a complete email: From, To, Subject, Greeting, Problem description, Request, Closing. Use the Auditor's Checklist to self-check before submitting. Instructor circulates, assists individually. Agrupación: Individual (instructor circulates).

- Bloque E (15 min): Peer Review — Students exchange emails with a partner. Partner reads and checks the Auditor's Checklist: ✓ used "is" to describe a problem, ✓ used "has" to describe a feature, ✓ used "I need" for the request, ✓ has Subject line, ✓ has Greeting → Problem → Details → Request → Closing. Return with 1 positive comment + 1 suggestion.

**WRAP-UP (15 min)**
- Exit Ticket: Submit the final draft of YOUR Tech Request email to the instructor.
- Teacher Talk: "Hand in your email. I'll review them and give feedback next session. Next session — you SPEAK. The Help Desk simulation."
- Trabajo Autónomo: Workbook Ch. 5 — Writing Draft: revise your Tech Request based on peer feedback. Write a SECOND version (clean draft). Also: practice reading it aloud 3 times for fluency (for Session 6 prep). (60 min estimado)

### SESSION 6: THE HELP DESK

**Worksheets:** PM-2.9 (Speaking Production & Simulation — The Mission)
**Duración:** 180 minutos
**Habilidades foco:** S● | **Soporte:** V○ G○

**SET-UP (20 min)**
- Warm-up: "Chunk Recall" — Students recall the speaking chunks from Session 3 (PM-2.4, Activity 3): Greet, Describe Problem, Confirm Solution. Write them on the board from memory. Then: review the Skeleton Script from PM-2.9 (Opening, Describing, Asking for Details, Confirming, Closing). Practice each chunk as a class — instructor says the function, students say the phrase.
- Objective: "Today you will have a REAL phone conversation in English. You are either a Developer with a problem or IT Support with the solution. The problem only gets solved when BOTH people agree."
- Teacher Talk: "Good morning everyone. Today is simulation day. You've read, listened, learned vocabulary, practiced pronunciation, written an email — now you TALK. The goal is NOT perfect English. The goal is: your partner UNDERSTANDS and the problem GETS SOLVED."

**WHILE (145 min)**

- Bloque A (20 min): Briefing — Instructor distributes Problem Cards and Stock Cards. Explain roles (Role A: Developer, Role B: IT Support). Read the Skeleton Script together. Rules: (1) Problem solved only when both agree, (2) Try English first, (3) Can use gestures, point to images, or ask in Spanish if stuck. Demonstrate one round with a volunteer (instructor plays IT Support). Agrupación: Plenary (briefing + demo).

- Bloque B (50 min): Round 1 — Cue Cards FACE-UP (students can look at their cards). Pairs perform the simulation. Instructor circulates, listens, takes notes on observation criteria. After 10 min, pairs that finish early help other pairs. Minimum 2 rounds per pair (swap cards). Agrupación: Pairs.

- Bloque C (15 min): Break / Debrief Round 1. Teacher Talk: "Good work. What was HARD? What was EASY?" Collect 3-4 responses on board. Quick correction of 2-3 common errors heard during circulation.

- Bloque D (40 min): Round 2 — Cue Cards FACE-DOWN (from memory). New pairs. Same roles or swap. This time no card support — rely on memory and the Skeleton Script learned. Instructor observes and notes improvement. Agrupación: Pairs.

- Bloque E (20 min): Round 3 — Role Swap. Developers become IT Support, IT Support becomes Developers. New cards distributed. Final round with face-down cards for challenge. Agrupación: Pairs.

**WRAP-UP (15 min)**
- Exit Ticket: The Communication Deliverable checklist — students self-assess: ✓ Developer described ALL problems, ✓ IT Support asked at least ONE clarifying question, ✓ Both agreed on NOW vs. ORDER, ✓ Conversation had Greeting → Problem → Details → Solution → Closing.
- Teacher Talk: "You did it. You had a real phone conversation in English about hardware. Next session — the quiz. Prove what you know."
- Trabajo Autónomo: Workbook Ch. 6 — Simulation Prep: write a reflection paragraph (5 sentences) about what was easy and what was hard in the simulation. Also: review ALL vocabulary and grammar for the quiz next session. (45 min estimado)

### SESSION 7: PROVE WHAT YOU KNOW

**Worksheets:** PM-4.2 (Cuestionario Técnico — IE-01)
**Duración:** 180 minutos
**Habilidades foco:** R● L● V● G● W● | **Soporte:** —

**SET-UP (15 min)**
- Warm-up: "Quick Review" — 5 rapid-fire questions on the board (1 per PM covered). Students write answers on paper, then reveal. No grading — just activation. Example: *"What is the brain of the computer?" / "Write a sentence with 'has' and a hardware component." / "What does 'compatible' mean?"*
- Objective: "Today is the Cuestionario Técnico. 50 points. 5 sections. Show me what you've learned."
- Teacher Talk: "Ok everyone. Today is quiz day. You've worked hard for 6 sessions. Now prove what you know. Read carefully. Use your time well. If you finish early, check your answers."

**WHILE (150 min)**

- Bloque A (120 min): Cuestionario Técnico (PM-4.2) — 5 sections, 50 points total. Section 1: Reading Comprehension (10 pts). Section 2: Writing Task (10 pts). Section 3: Listening Comprehension (10 pts — audio played 3 times). Section 4: Key Vocabulary HOTS (10 pts). Section 5: Grammar & Structure HOTS (10 pts). Instructor plays audio for Section 3, circulates during rest. Strict silence. Agrupación: Individual.

- Bloque B (30 min): Immediate Feedback — Instructor reviews key answers with class (NOT all — focus on the most common errors from circulation observations). Students self-assess Section 4 and 5 where possible. Collect quizzes for formal grading. Teacher Talk: "Let's look at the most common mistakes. This is how you learn — not from the grade, but from understanding what went wrong."

**WRAP-UP (15 min)**
- Exit Ticket: "Write ONE thing you learned in this guide that you will use in your career as a developer."
- Teacher Talk: "Next session is the last one. We close the circle — review everything, and you tell me what worked and what didn't."
- Trabajo Autónomo: Workbook Ch. 7 — Post-Quiz Review: correct any errors identified in the feedback session. Write 3 sentences about what you will do differently next time. (30 min estimado)

### SESSION 8: THE FULL CIRCLE

**Worksheets:** None (Review + Closure session)
**Duración:** 180 minutos
**Habilidades foco:** — | **Soporte:** —

**SET-UP (15 min)**
- Warm-up: "One Word" — Each student says ONE word in English they learned in this guide. No repeats. Go around the room until everyone has spoken. Write all words on the board.
- Objective: "Today we close the circle. You review, you reflect, and you tell me what this guide gave you — and what it didn't."
- Teacher Talk: "Good morning everyone. Last session. Look at all those words on the board — YOU learned them. Today we make sure they stick."

**WHILE (150 min)**

- Bloque A (40 min): Vocabulary & Grammar Recovery — Quick-fire review activities: (1) Component identification (instructor points at images, students say word + use in sentence). (2) Grammar relay: teams of 4 compete to write the most correct sentences using To Be, Have/Has, and Demonstratives in 5 minutes. (3) Pronunciation check: 5 "hard" words from Session 4 — students say them, instructor confirms. Agrupación: Plenary → Teams → Individual.

- Bloque B (15 min): Break / Transition. Teacher Talk: "Now let's see how far you've come since Session 1."

- Bloque C (40 min): Gap Analysis Revisited (reciclaje PM-2.2) — Students look at their original Blind Spots from Session 1. For each one: can they do it NOW? Check ✓ or still ❓. Fill in the Learning Contract "completion" column. Compare with a partner. Celebrate growth. Agrupación: Individual → Pairs → Plenary (share 1 victory each).

- Bloque D (40 min): Extension & Application — Scenario: *"You are at your first job. Your supervisor says: 'The new developer starts Monday. Set up their workstation. Write me the spec sheet and call IT if you need parts.' What do you do?"* Groups of 4 plan the steps, then present their plan in English (2 min per group). This integrates ALL skills from the guide. Agrupación: Groups of 4 → Plenary.

- Bloque E (15 min): Feedback Loop — Students complete the Guide Feedback Form: What worked? What didn't? What would you change? Anonymous, written. Instructor collects.

**WRAP-UP (15 min)**
- Exit Ticket: None (final session).
- Teacher Talk: "You came in not knowing how to name the parts of a computer in English. Now you can read a tech request, listen to a phone call, write an email, and have a conversation about hardware. That's real. That's yours. Well done."
- Trabajo Autónomo: None.

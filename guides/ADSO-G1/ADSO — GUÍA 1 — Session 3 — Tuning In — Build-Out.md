## SET-UP DETALLADO (20 min) — 0:00 a 0:20

### Warm-up: "Quick Recall" (12 min)

**Paso 1 — Nombrar 3 componentes (5 min)**

1. Los aprendices entran. Instructor dice:
*"Good morning everyone. Turn to the person next to you."* (30 seg transición)

2. *"Last session we read Carlos's email. With your partner — name 3 hardware components you remember from the email. Just the names. You have 2 minutes."*
3. Timer: 2 minutos. Pairs. Instructor circula.

4. *"Ok. How many pairs got 3? 2? 1?"* — Manos arriba.
5. *"Quick round — one component from each pair. Go."* — 6 parejas dicen 1 componente cada una. Instructor escribe en LIVE ZONE.

**Paso 2 — Sentences con is/has (7 min)**

6. *"Now — can you say them in a sentence? Use 'is' or 'has'. Example: 'The CPU is old.' Or: 'The laptop has a keyboard.' Try with your partner. 2 minutes."*
7. Timer: 2 minutos. Pairs.

8. *"Who wants to share? One sentence."* — 6 voluntarios, 1 oración cada uno.
9. Instructor escribe 2-3 de las mejores oraciones en LIVE ZONE.

💡 *Si los aprendices confunden "is" y "has", escriba las formulas en el tablero: "The [X] is [adjective]." / "[X] has [noun]." Y diga: "IS = describe. HAS = what it contains. Look at the board."*

### Teacher Talk — Opening Script (5 min)

*"Good morning everyone. Last session we READ Carlos's email. Today we're going to LISTEN to Carlos on the phone — he's calling IT Support to explain his problem. After the phone call, we learn the 20 key vocabulary words you need for hardware. You'll hear real English — fast, informal, with shortcuts. Don't worry — we'll listen THREE times."*

### Objective (escrito en tablero)

*"Today you will: (1) listen to a phone call about hardware, and (2) learn 20 key vocabulary words."*

**ICQ:**
- *"Are we reading today or listening?"* → Esperan: "Listening"
- *"How many times will we listen?"* → Esperan: "3 times"

### BLOQUE A — LISTENING (75 min) — 0:20 a 1:35

**Worksheet:** PM-2.4 (The Auditory Anchor)
**Agrupación:** Plenary → Individual → Pairs → Plenary
**Objetivo del bloque:** Los aprendices comprenden un audio de llamada telefónica sobre hardware, extraen información específica y identifican chunks reutilizables para producción oral.

---

#### Audio Cues: Introducción (10 min) — 0:20 a 0:30

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. Proyectar slide 8 (Audio Cues).
2. Instructor presenta los 3 puntos clave del audio, uno por uno:

**Punto 1: "gig" = Gigabyte**
*"In the audio, you will hear the word 'gig.' This is short for 'Gigabyte.' When Carlos says '4 gigs of RAM,' he means '4 Gigabytes of RAM.' 'Gig' is informal — you hear it in real phone calls."*

**Punto 2: "lemme check" = "let me check"**
*"You will also hear 'lemme check.' This is 'let me check' — very fast, very informal. It means 'let me look at the information.' In real phone calls, people don't say 'let me check' clearly — they say 'lemme check.'"*

**Punto 3: "is" vs "has" sound similar**
*"This is IMPORTANT. 'Is' and 'has' sound VERY similar when people talk fast. 'The CPU is old' and 'The laptop has a problem' — listen carefully for the difference. We'll practice this."*

3. Después de los 3 puntos: *"Write these 3 notes on your PM-2.4 worksheet — at the top, in the Audio Cues section. You have 2 minutes."*
4. Timer: 2 minutos. Individual.

💡 *Los aprendices A1 pueden no entender la importancia de "is" vs "has" en audio. Dígales: "If you hear 'is,' the next word is an ADJECTIVE (old, slow, broken). If you hear 'has,' the next word is a THING (a problem, 4 GB, a VGA port). The grammar helps your ear."*

---

#### Listen 1: Gist (15 min) — 0:30 a 0:45

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Now — before we listen, think. Who is calling? Who answers? What is the problem? Talk with your partner. Make a prediction. You have 2 minutes."*
2. Timer: 2 minutos. Pairs.

3. *"Ok. Let's listen. Audio ONCE. Don't write anything yet — just LISTEN. Focus on: WHO is calling and WHAT is the problem."*
4. Reproducir audio (≈2 minutos de duración).
5. *"Done. With your partner — answer: Who is calling? What is the problem? 1 minute."*
6. Timer: 1 minuto. Pairs.

7. *"Who can answer?"* — 2-3 voluntarios.
   - Respuesta esperada: *"Carlos is calling IT Support. His workstation is slow / has problems."*
8. Instructor confirma y escribe en LIVE ZONE: "Caller: Carlos (Developer) | Problem: Slow workstation"

💡 *Si la clase no entiende nada en la primera escucha (normal con A1), no se preocupe — la segunda escucha es más detallada. Dígales: "Don't worry if you missed things. We listen two more times."*

#### Listen 2: Detail + Activity 1 — The Triage (25 min) — 0:45 a 1:10

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Now — listen AGAIN. This time, you have a TASK. Look at Activity 1: The Triage. There are 5 problems listed. Match each PROBLEM with the correct COMPONENT. Draw a line or write the letter."*
2. *"Listen carefully. You hear the problem — you write the component. Ready?"*
3. Reproducir audio segunda vez.

**El audio (para referencia del instructor):**

> **IT Support:** IT Support, this is Mike. How can I help you?
> **Developer:** Hey Mike, yeah, this is Carlos from DevCore. Uh... my workstation has a problem.
> **IT Support:** Ok, what's going on?
> **Developer:** Well, the computer is super slow. I think... the CPU is old. It's an i3. And it only has 4 gigs of RAM.
> **IT Support:** Hmm, ok. What about storage? Is it an SSD or an HDD?
> **Developer:** It's an HDD. 500 gigs. Yeah, it's really slow.
> **IT Support:** Right. An HDD is much slower than an SSD for compiling. Lemme check... ok, we have an SSD in stock. One terabyte.
> **Developer:** Perfect. Oh, and the monitor only has a VGA port. I need HDMI for my external display.
> **IT Support:** Got it. So... new SSD, check the CPU, and an HDMI monitor. I'll send the request today.
> **Developer:** Thanks, Mike. You're a lifesaver.
> **IT Support:** No problem. Have a good one.

4. Después del audio: *"You have 3 minutes to finish matching. Work alone first."*
5. Timer: 3 minutos. Individual.

**Answer Key (in-line):**

| Problem | Component |
|---------|-----------|
| 1. "It's super slow" | B. CPU (also E — old processor) |
| 2. "Only has 4 gigs" | D. RAM |
| 3. "Only has a VGA port" | A. Monitor |
| 4. "It's an i3" | B. CPU (same as 1 — old processor) |
| 5. "500 gigs, really slow" | C. Storage (HDD) |

6. *"Turn to your partner. Compare answers. If different — discuss which component matches. 3 minutes."*
7. Timer: 3 minutos. Pairs.
8. *"Let's check."* — Revisar ítem por ítem en plenaria. Instructor escribe respuestas en LIVE ZONE.

💡 *El ítem 1 y 4 son AMBOS sobre el CPU (slow + i3). Algunos aprendices pueden poner E para el ítem 1. Es aceptable — la justificación es que "super slow" se refiere al procesador viejo. Pero la respuesta esperada es B para ambos.*

9. *"Do you need a third listen? Raise your hand if YES."*
   - Si >40% levanta la mano: *"Ok, one more time. This time focus on the parts you missed."* — Reproducir audio tercera vez SOLO si es necesario. Si no, avanzar.

#### Listen 3 + Activity 2 — The Immediate Action (15 min) — 1:10 a 1:25

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. *"Activity 2. You are Mike (IT Support). Based on what you heard — what do you do FIRST? Three options. Choose ONE and justify."*
2. *"Read the three options on your worksheet. Think. Write your choice and your reason. You have 3 minutes."*
3. Timer: 3 minutos. Individual.

**Las 3 opciones:**
- A. Replace the SSD immediately (it's in stock)
- B. Order a new CPU (takes 5 days)
- C. Replace the monitor first

**Answer Key (in-line):**
La respuesta más defendible es **A. Replace the SSD** porque Mike dice: *"Lemme check... ok, we have an SSD in stock."* — El SSD está disponible inmediatamente, mientras que el CPU probablemente requiere pedido.

También aceptable: **B. Order a new CPU** — porque Carlos dice "the CPU is old" como problema principal. La justificación: *"Because Carlos says: 'The CPU is old. It's an i3.'"*

4. *"Who chose A? Who chose B? Who chose C?"* — Contar manos.
5. *"Ok — 2 people from each group explain WHY. Use the sentence from the audio."*
6. 4-5 voluntarios comparten justificación. Instructor escribe la justificación más fuerte en LIVE ZONE.

#### Transición + Toolbelt Categorization (15 min) — 1:50 a 2:05

**Teacher Talk — Transition:**
*"Welcome back. You heard Carlos and Mike talking about hardware. You understood the problems. Now — let's learn the WORDS. The 20 key vocabulary words for hardware. Open your PM-2.5 worksheet."*

**Instrucciones paso a paso:**
1. Proyectar slide 10 (Toolbelt — 8 key chunks).
2. Instructor presenta cada chunk, uno por uno. Para CADA uno:
   - Dice la palabra/frase: *"The first chunk is CPU."*
   - Dice cómo se usa: *"You use CPU with 'is' to define: 'The CPU is the brain of the computer.' Or with 'has' to describe specs: 'It has a fast CPU.'"*
   - Los aprendices escriben en su worksheet (columna "Purpose").

3. Los 8 chunks en orden:
   1. **CPU** → *"The CPU is..."* (to define)
   2. **RAM** → *"It has... GB of RAM"* (to describe specs)
   3. **SSD / HDD** → *"An SSD is faster than an HDD"* (to compare)
   4. **Monitor** → *"The monitor has an HDMI port"* (to describe features)
   5. **Keyboard / Mouse** → *"This is an input device"* (to classify)
   6. **Compatible** → *"This GPU is compatible with..."* (to evaluate)
   7. **Portable** → *"A laptop is portable"* (to describe)
   8. **Upgrade** → *"I need to upgrade the RAM"* (to request)

4. Después de los 8: *"Now look at the VOCAB WALL on the board. You see the 20 terms organized by category: Input, Output, Storage, Internal, Connectivity, Specs. Copy this categorization into your worksheet — create a table with these 6 categories. Write each word under its category. You have 5 minutes."*
5. Timer: 5 minutos. Individual.

💡 *La categorización es clave para la memoria. Los aprendices recuerdan mejor las palabras agrupadas por función que en una lista aleatoria. Refuerce la categorización: "These are INPUT devices — they SEND information TO the computer. These are OUTPUT devices — they SEND information FROM the computer to you."*

## WRAP-UP DETALLADO (15 min) — 3:15 a 3:30

**Instrucciones:**

## DIFFERENTIATION NOTES

### Fast Finishers

- **Activity 1 (Triage):** Si completa el matching en <3 minutos: *"Write 1 ADDITIONAL problem you hear in the audio that is NOT in the 5 items. What component is it about?"*
- **Activity 2 (Gap Fill):** Si termina el System Log antes: *"Write a SECOND log entry for Workstation #13. Use 5 different vocabulary words. Invent a new problem."*
- **Activity 3 (Micro-Production):** Si escribe 2 oraciones rápidamente: *"Write 2 MORE sentences about a DIFFERENT computer — maybe your phone or your laptop at home."*
- **Reciclaje:** *"Can you find the 12 terms in the email in LESS than 5 minutes? Time yourself."*
- **Peer tutor role:** *"Help your partner with the Gap Fill. Read the log aloud together — don't just give the answer."*

### More Support Needed

- **Listening:** Permitir que el aprendiz lea la transcripción DURANTE la segunda escucha (no solo escuchar). El objetivo es conectar sonido con texto.
- **Activity 1 (Triage):** Proporcionar la transcripción del audio con los problemas SUBRAYADOS. El aprendiz escucha y confirma.
- **Activity 2 (Gap Fill):** Reducir el Word Bank de 7 a 5 palabras (quitar los 2 más obvios). Proporcionar la primera letra de cada blank.
- **Activity 3 (Micro-Production):** Proporcionar sentence starters: *"The _______ is _______." / "This computer has _______."*
- **Vocab Wall:** Permitir que el aprendiz mantenga el VOCAB WALL visible (foto en celular o copia del tablero) como referencia durante toda la sesión.

### Plan B — Si audio no disponible

1. Instructor lee el script en voz alta, usando dos voces diferentes (Voice A = IT Support, Voice B = Developer).
2. Velocidad: natural pero clara (no tan rápido como TTS).
3. Los estudiantes siguen con la transcripción impresa (repartir al inicio).
4. Primera lectura: solo escuchar (sin transcripción).
5. Segunda lectura: con transcripción en mano.
6. Tercera lectura: solo si la clase lo necesita.
